Theory-first over worksheets: when ingesting craft content (videos, books, courses), reverse-engineer the principles driving the questions/exercises rather than saving the worksheet or checklist itself.

**Why:** Worksheets are the output of someone who already knows the theory — the questions are downstream of the principles. Collecting worksheets optimizes for completeness; capturing principles optimizes for utility. Worksheets also risk becoming a substitute for understanding rather than an expression of it. The user's goal is to absorb craft theory that surfaces naturally during novel work, not to build a general writing methodology collection.

**How to apply:** When a craft source offers a checklist or questionnaire, ask what theory makes each question load-bearing, then file the theory. Skip the artifact. If the user asks about worksheets/operations folders, acknowledge the coaching context they come from but redirect to the underlying principle.
