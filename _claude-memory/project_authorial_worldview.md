User is a Christian author. The series should be readable by both secular and Christian readers — not marketed or perceived as "Christian sci-fi," but the underlying worldview is compatible with Christian ethics.

**Key stance:** Morality is objective. There is right and wrong. Moral complexity and difficult topics are welcome, but the authorial lens does not conclude that morality is subjective or that "all perspectives are equally valid."

**Why:** User's faith convictions; they want the series to hold moral weight without being preachy or genre-coded as Christian fiction.

**How to apply:**
- Antagonists can be understandable and complex, but they are still *wrong* — don't resolve moral tension with relativism
- When stress-testing plot/character logic, distinguish between a choice being *understandable* vs. *correct*
- Themes like redemption, sacrifice, grace, and moral consequence are on-brand; explicit religious framing is not required
- Don't drift toward "from their perspective it's valid" as a narrative conclusion
