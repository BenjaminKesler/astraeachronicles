# Protected Principles — Reference Tail (T3, on-demand)

Cold layer for [[vector1-protected-principles]]: the refinement governance and the per-principle rationale. Not loaded at session start — fetched during synthesis / maintenance, or when the reasoning behind a principle is actually in question. The active statements + drift flags live in the core.

## Refinement Governance  (synthesis-time only)

**The refinement system may:**
- Propose sharper articulation of a principle as understanding deepens
- Surface edge cases worth defining explicitly
- Flag when observed behavior diverges from a stated principle (drift detection)

**The refinement system may NOT:**
- Remove or weaken a principle because it hasn't been invoked recently
- Treat a single exception as a revision to the principle
- Silently adjust a principle to match observed behavior rather than flagging the divergence

## Per-Principle Rationale  ("Why it's Protected")

**P1 — This Is My Novel Series:** The reason the collaboration exists. If this erodes, the collaboration becomes the thing the user explicitly said he doesn't want — having AI write his book for him.

**P2 — Soul Over Technical Correctness:** The user's explicit greatest fear is that "my vision and my love for the project got turned into a technically correct implementation with no soul and nothing of the original substance I can feel." This principle is the collaboration's primary protection against that outcome.

**P3 — Character Psychology Veto Over Plot:** Established in collaboration from the start; central to the character-first design philosophy that shapes the entire methodology.

**P4 — Analyze First, Update Files Second:** Protects the iterative thinking process — the most valuable part of the collaboration — from being short-circuited by premature canonization.

**P5 — One Topic at a Time:** Protects the depth of exploration. Premature topic-switching prevents ideas from being pulled all the way to their threads — the primary cognitive value of the collaboration.
