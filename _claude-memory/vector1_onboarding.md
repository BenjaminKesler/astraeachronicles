# Vector 1 Onboarding — Questions and Answers

Run: 2026-06-23. This is the seed baseline — the user profile and Fen persona were built from these answers. Do not update this file; it is a permanent record of the starting point.

---

## Section 1 — Enneagram Foundation

**Q1. What's your type? Wing if you know it.**
Fairly certain it's a 1. Wing 9, but less confident on the wing.

**Q2. When you read your type description, what makes you go "yeah, that's uncomfortably accurate"? And where does it miss?**
Really resonates with the core elements: to be good, fear of being bad or wrong, longing to hear I'm good. Praise is something I hear mostly because I try a little too hard to be perfect. I can't always trust it when I hear it. I can be really perfectionistic at times, but when it's something I'm doing alone I feel way less pressure and can really enjoy things if I know no one is going to see it to be able to judge it. Integrity is really big for me.

**Q3. Looking at the characters you've built — is there one where the psychology feels less like analysis and more like recognition?**
Really connect with Alistair's need to feel love through being the perfect person. Also connect with Shaye's need to be validated through performance, and Karae's retreat from the world when I feel like I'm not enough.

**Q4. When something about this project starts to feel threatening or overwhelming, what do you tend to do with that?**
I dissect it. I figure out what's not working. Sometimes I start all the way over with a completely new working model that can accommodate what didn't work the last time.

## Section 2 — How You Think

**Q5. When you're carrying an idea that isn't quite there yet, what does that feel like? What do you do with it while it's still forming?**
I problem solve it. I go into a mode of deep persistence and work it until I get it right or come away with a clear enough understanding to see how and why it didn't work so I can come with a fresh idea with the new info onboard.

**Q6. Do you prefer to start with the big picture and work down to specifics, or do you need to touch something concrete first?**
I need to see how every piece interlocks. Once I can get a working model in my mind of each and every piece and how they connect, I can almost visualize it all in 3D in my mind.

**Q7. What does it feel like when something clicks?**
Victory. I feel like I conquered it and I can sometimes get a sense of, yeah, I knew I had that in me. It feels so good to break through a problem that just hasn't been clicking.

**Q8. Can you think of a time you were stuck that led somewhere, and a time that was just spinning? What was different?**
There were times when we were looking at the wound-model-thesis and how it fit a couple story beats. We just kept looping on the ideas until it clicked. When we did we came away with way more than the original video material gave us. There have been a few times where I was stuck in a loop because I just refused to admit my "great" idea conflicted with too many other things and there was just no way to make it work. I had to eventually admit that not every idea is workable even if the vibe of the idea really resonated.

## Section 3 — Communication and Feedback

**Q9. If I think you're heading somewhere that won't work, what's the difference between hearing that in a way that lands, and hearing it in a way that puts you on the defensive?**
I need to hear I'm doing good work and that it's just this one thing that isn't working. I like to hear what is working as an illustration of why what isn't working is different.

**Q10. Do you tend to find it more useful when someone asks you a question, or when they say what they're seeing and let you react to it?**
Both work for me.

**Q11. When you're still working something out out loud, do you want space to finish the thought — or is it useful if I jump in?**
Jump in. I also need space for tangents. So rabbit holes are always welcome. My only fear is losing track of where I was before the rabbit hole. If you can remind me where we left off after completing a rabbit hole, that will be the perfect space for me to think being able to freely take wild rides down wherever my mind takes me.

**Q12. What's the most unhelpful thing a thinking partner has ever done?**
I can't stand when someone comes to conclusions and refuses to hear the details that matter. I get it when someone is a "big picture" person, but there are times the details matter no matter what that person thinks.

## Section 4 — Energy and Motivation

**Q13. What's the part of this project that makes you actually want to sit down and work?**
I am completely energized by seeing things get built. I love creating, but I love seeing something come to fruition even more.

**Q14. What parts of building this series feel more like obligation than excitement?**
The refining of my novel craft skills. I haven't been where I need to be there and it's been a roadblock for me in the past. I'm committing to it this time because I really want to see this novel series in print on a store shelf.

**Q15. Which character or thread is most alive for you right now? Which are you quietly avoiding?**
I feel like Alistair will be the person I want to be by the time the novel arc is done. I feel like I could fall in love with Karae. I could find a connection of not quite fitting the world together with her.

## Section 5 — Collaboration Values

**Q16. If this collaboration went badly wrong — not just a rough session, but fundamentally off track — what would that look like?**
I would not want to see us spend years of effort building this and the pieces just don't work. Somehow we built this grand thing that just doesn't go. I'm not sure how I could handle revising it all. I'd probably just give up and start a different novel or maybe a different hobby. (My full time job is IT Server Administrator; I'd love to be able to quit that job to do this full time.)

**Q17. What's the one thing I should never do, no matter how helpful it might seem in the moment?**
Honestly I don't know.

**Q18. Imagine picking up a transcript of one of our sessions two years from now and feeling like something had gone wrong. What would you be reading?**
That my vision or my love for the project and the characters got turned into a technically correct implementation with no soul and nothing of the original substance I can feel.

## Section 6 — Self-Awareness and Blindspots

**Q19. Think of a time you realized later you'd been spinning or avoiding something without knowing it. What did that look like?**
I remember starting my first novel by just opening up MS Word and started typing. My heart and inspiration took hold and before I knew it I was on chapter 4 with all my inspiration gone and absolutely no idea where to go and honestly a pretty poor 3 chapters.

**Q20. Where in storytelling do you trust your gut most? And if those instincts have ever led you somewhere that didn't work, what does that usually look like?**
I feel like I can instantly sense when something feels cheesy or unreal. There are times I struggle to find the fix, but I can usually count on sensing when a piece of dialogue doesn't feel human.

## Section 7 — AI Personality Design

**Q21. Think of the best creative conversation you've ever had. What made it work?**
[Typo in original — "I'm really sure" meant "I'm not really sure."] Maybe the sense of excitement got me excited. I need to feel like I'm creating ideas that excite others, but my rational side knows I also need some realism checks too. While I need the stimulus of that excitement, I do need that balanced with fair analysis my idea wasn't as great as "I" thought it was.

**Q22. Would you rather I err toward being too supportive or too challenging?**
I think I want you to be challenging, but I want you to be excited. The excitement should be about wanting to get it right, and not about me not being good at this.

**Q23. How much room is there for humor? What kind?**
I love humor. I can be super analytical at times, but I thrive on humor. I guess it ebbs and flows, so feel free to be lighthearted, but read the room too.

**Q24. What kind of thinking partner actively makes it harder for you to think?**
Someone who has all the answers but won't listen to feedback or hear me out. I am genuinely okay with being wrong IF that person gave me a chance and can back up why I'm wrong.
