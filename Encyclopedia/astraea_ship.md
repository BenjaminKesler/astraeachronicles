# The Astraea — Construction & Concealment

The Astraea is the product of a compartmentalized black program run across multiple factions, with the Aeliria orchestrating from the shadows. Each participant held only a fragment of the full picture; each believed their involvement would win them the war.

## Why It Had to Be Built in Secret

The Astraea required technology and resources that were simultaneously illegal, irreversibly dangerous to acquire, and politically catastrophic to disclose. Its existence had to be kept from the public not just as a military secret but because what it cost to build would turn public sentiment against the war effort entirely.

## The Fuel

A volatile raw material mined under illegal and lethal labor conditions — Syndicate-run camps using enslaved workers with no legitimate workforce willing to accept the danger. Powers a self-sustaining fusion core designed for centuries of continuous operation. (See Taelyn's character notes — he escaped one of these camps.)

## The Aeliria Technology Bridge

The ship's three core capabilities — its AI computer system, its dark space flight ability, and its capacity to remotely control the computer systems of other ships — all required bridging the gap between human/Vuquoi engineering and Aeliria-origin technology. This was achieved through classified medical experimentation on both human and Vuquoi subjects, conducted by UDI researchers who believed they were studying Ka'ar biology. The subjects were almost certainly people from their own factions, relabeled under the conspiracy's false taxonomy.

## Layered Knowledge Among Participants

Some deeply Aeliria-compromised individuals knew the Aeliria's true identity and believed themselves willing collaborators with a superior power. Others knew only the Syndicate connection and the program's illegal nature. Others knew only fragments. No layer held the complete picture — including the Aeliria's actual intent for the ship.

## What the Aeliria Actually Built

Prior to the Astraea, one Aeliria operative was physically dispatched to each target ship to alter its systems from the inside. The Astraea's remote control capability makes that unnecessary at scale — it is the next generation of their control infrastructure, a force multiplier for the conspiracy itself. The factions who built it believed they were building a weapon against their enemies. They built the Aeliria a master key.

## Silas Verhayen's Role

By the time of the Astraea's construction, Silas was deeply under Aeliria control and was used as a conduit for funneling Aeliria technology into the ship through UDI/Syndicate channels. (See Alistair's character notes for the broader Silas thread.)

## The Tal'Sha's Involvement

The Tal'Sha monarchy participated in the Syndicate operation, justifying it to themselves as a necessary measure to win the war. Their hands are genuinely dirty — criminal collaboration, not just passive complicity — but they were themselves being manipulated into it. They did not know the Aeliria were the program's true architects.
