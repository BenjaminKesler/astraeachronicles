🛡️ Alistair: The Institutional Pillar

> _"If the system is righteous, then my hands are clean. I will not let the legacy of my family be tarnished by your chaos."_

- **Enneagram:** **Type 1 (The Reformer) with a 2 Wing**
- **The Overarching Theme Mirror:** He uses rigid institutional protocol to bury the truth of his own complicity in a horrific, eternal war.
- **Backstory & History:** Raised as the golden child of an elite human military dynasty. His father is a celebrated war hero of the UDI — one of the great men of the human empire who put everything into defending it — and an Alliance Admiral who has meticulously engineered Alistair's trajectory for leadership. Alistair has spent his entire life suppressing his personal desires, anger, and instincts to be the "perfect instrument" of the Alliance.
- **The Internal Lie / Misbelief:** _"If I am perfectly compliant with the chain of command and maintain absolute moral perfection, I am blameless. The system protects my morality."_
- **The Vulnerable Virtue (Why Readers Love Him):** While he is stiff and unyielding to his superiors, he is fiercely protective of his lower-ranking crew. He secretly steps in to take the blame and shield underdogs from corrupt senior officers. His perfectionism is driven by a deep, distorted desire to be a shield for the weak.
- **The Core Conflict & Exploitation:** The Aeliria exploit his need for order. When he encounters data suggesting the Alliance is corrupt, his brain violently rejects it—manifesting as crushing migraines as his mind edits the data out to preserve his worldview.
- **The Turning Point & Breaking the Illusion:** He discovers an un-spoofed memory log showing that an orbital strike he personally commanded years ago—which he was told hit a Ka'ar military stronghold—actually destroyed a Vuqtai civilian settlement. The target was spoofed by the Aeliria. His father authorized the strike. _(Roan's family was killed in this strike. Neither Alistair nor Roan knows the other is connected to it — the reveal is intended to land later in the story.)_
- **The Ultimate Epiphany:** True honor requires destroying a corrupt structure, not preserving it. He defects, stripping away his heavy military armor, and accepts his horrific guilt. He joins _The Astraea_ to find the encrypted message his father left hidden in the ship's code before the Aeliria completely hollowed the man out.

## Key Relationships

- **Karae:** She sees him as the human embodiment of everything she holds the Tal'Sha accountable for — a person with a genuine internal code of honor who has surrendered it entirely to an institution. He initially reads her refusal of institutional loyalty as dishonor, not principle. Both are right about each other's flaw and wrong about each other's character. Over time each teaches the other: she forces him to question the structure; he shows her that commitment to people isn't the same as compliance to power. Late-series layer: Silas's role as an Aeliria-controlled conduit for the Astraea connects Alistair's family history directly to the Tal'Sha's criminal involvement in the program — and by extension to the death of Karae's brother, who was erased for getting too close to that connection.
- **Callum:** Callum envies Alistair's "perfect" military life, only to realize that perfection was a blind compliance loop. Callum teaches Alistair to question the system; Alistair teaches Callum to stand firm.
- **Roan:** Roan's family was killed in the orbital strike Alistair commanded — authorized by Silas, executed by Alistair. Neither knows the other is connected to it. The reveal is intended to land later in the story.
