⚙️ Shaye: The Transactional Identity

> _"My flesh is weak, but my upgrades are perfect. I am exactly what the mission needs me to be. Without my utility, I am nothing."_

- **Enneagram:** **Type 3 (The Achiever) with a 4 Wing**
- **The Overarching Theme Mirror:** She replaces her biological soul with mechanical utility, believing the manufactured lie of her upgrades is superior to the messy truth of her humanity.
- **Backstory & History:** An orphan of the war who survived by turning herself into a corporate asset for the Syndicate, slowly trading her organic body parts for bleeding-edge military cybernetics. She became a legendary, unfeeling spy embedded with the Vuquoi rebels, sent to infiltrate and capture _The Astraea_ — believing it to be a Ka'ar weapon, not a ship crewed by people.
- **The Internal Lie / Misbelief:** _"I am only worth what I can produce. My cybernetic upgrades and flawless execution are the only things that give me value."_
- **The Vulnerable Virtue (Why Readers Love Her):** Despite her cold, calculated exterior, she maintains a secret "orphan fund" and uses her data-theft to quietly delete execution orders for civilian whistleblowers. She fiercely hates the corruption that stole her childhood and fights desperately to protect other innocents from the same fate.
- **The Core Conflict & Exploitation:** The Aeliria exploit her drive for efficiency through her integrated SAVI AI chip, using it to tailor her memories and track _The Astraea_. She believes that turning off the chip will make her a helpless, invisible nobody.
- **The Turning Point & Breaking the Illusion:** Her data signature compromises the crew, leading to their capture. Her rebel faction betrays her, branding her a traitor. To save them, she is forced to manually shut down her cybernetics and disconnect the AI.
- **The Ultimate Epiphany:** Value is intrinsic, not performance-based. Hit with ten years of delayed, screaming childhood PTSD all at once due to the AI's absence, she executes a desperate, messy rescue mission while weeping and shaking from sensory withdrawal. She relies purely on raw human grit, proving she was always the weapon—not the machine.
