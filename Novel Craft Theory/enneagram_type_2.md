# TYPE 2 — HEART · giving-to-get

WOUND: love = earned through giving, not freely received → self-worth borrowed from others' need. Conscious self-image is warm and certain: "I am a gift" — the pride is fierce and largely invisible. NOT aware of the transaction beneath the gift.
MECH [the ledger]: every gift is a ledger entry; books are kept unconsciously. "After all I did for you" = threshold marker that the ledger has tipped past containment.
MECH [indirection]: cannot ask for love directly — to ask is to admit need = to hand someone the ability to say no. Circles the love through elaborate maneuvers. This produces both genuine warmth AND the shadow (manipulation, resentment).
ARC: authentic self-esteem vs. borrowed self-worth. The what can stay the same; only the why changes — giving from abundance ≠ giving from scarcity.
ENGINE: accumulation of unreturned gifts → eruption surprises everyone including the two (surface was so warm so long)
TENSION: pursuing love through a strategy that structurally prevents receiving it in the form they need — gratitude for service ≠ being loved for who they are
STRESS→8: contract breaks → warm exterior → rage/domination. Interior = devastation of a scorned lover/abandoned child; exterior = mob enforcer. The dichotomy is the dramatic reveal.
INT→4: genuine self-expression; authentic self turns out to be lovable

SHAPES
  redeem: boundaries (choose self even if other person hates them for it) | motivation shift (behavior same, why changes — hardest to dramatize) | inverse action (the arc-completing act is the antithesis of the fixated behavior) | false charity/disillusionment (first moment of genuine honesty → grief, not defeat)
  tragedy: never stops giving-to-get → erupts or collapses → alone, still certain they're the most loving person in the room — self-image is last thing to go
  antag: control through care. "It's for your own good." lord of the little domain | possessive caretaker (creates the need they fill) | smothering protector

ANTAG weakness: object of care choosing genuine independence + receiving love elsewhere. Cannot be defeated by argument (reframes it as ingratitude). Only what breaks them: the beloved demonstrating they don't actually need them.

NOT
  → resentment = equal to the warmth — it is the structural result of giving-to-get; every unreturned gift is a stone on a pile
  → high self-opinion ("I give more than most people are capable of giving") — the two who feels overlooked thinks "how dare they," not "maybe I don't deserve it"
  → giving is NOT purely selfless — warmth is genuine AND there's a transaction. Both true simultaneously. Not exposing them as fraud; showing the mechanism in someone who cannot see it.
  → NOT doormats — absorb strategically; transform into martyrdom or explode toward eight
  → male twos violate cultural expectations → neediness disguised as domination; emotional hunger expressed as rage; richest dramatic territory in the type

DX
  1. What do they believe about themselves at their most sincere — private self-assessment, where does the pride live?
  2. What do they need to hear that they've never been able to ask for — the specific sentence they cannot say?
  3. What is the ledger — what gifts and care do they track without knowing they track them?
  4. Who do they most need to need them — what happens if that person stopped?
  5. When have they engineered someone's dependency on them — did they know? What was the mechanism?
  6. What does their resentment look like when it finally surfaces?
  7. Where does the indirect-asking strategy show up — what specific maneuvers do they use?
  8. What happens when their giving produces the wrong result — when the person they helped becomes independent?
  9. What is the authentic self beneath the caregiver — what do they want/prefer/feel that has nothing to do with anyone else's needs?
  10. When they move toward eight in stress — what triggers it? Who are they when the warmth breaks?
  11. What would it cost them to ask for love directly — what do they imagine happening?
  12. What does integration look like — the moment they give from abundance rather than scarcity?
