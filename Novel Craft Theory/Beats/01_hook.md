# Hook — Act 1 Beat 1
# source: "How to Write a Hook For Your Story" — Abbie Emmons — https://youtu.be/s7a6R5lVW3g
# source: expert synthesis — Cron (Story Genius), Weiland (Creating Character Arcs), Hauge (Hero's 2 Journeys), Snyder (Save the Cat), McKee (Story)
# cross-references: [[internal-conflict]], [[wound-protective-mechanisms]], [[backstory-exposition]], [[universal-diagnostics]], [[plot-structure-outlining]]

---

WOUND LAYER (the heart)
state: wound already happened; misbelief operational; surface desire in active pursuit; dominant mechanism at full strength
  surface desire + dominant mechanism visible → reader infers misbelief without it being named
  abstract desire: subterranean — NOT established here; reserved for aha moment payoff
    Hauge nuance: abstract desire may leak as incongruity (glimpse of essence through identity); latent, never stated
  internal conflict MUST pre-exist the II
    if not: II matters only abstractly — not to this character's specific wound
    reader has no wound-context through which to receive the II; it becomes a plot event, not a story event
  dominant mechanism at FULL STRENGTH — non-negotiable
    this is the floor the arc degrades from across Acts 1–2
    mechanism already cracking at the hook = arc has nowhere to go
    → [[wound-protective-mechanisms]] for dominant mechanism baseline and arc diagnostic

FAILURE MODE: SURVIVAL MODE
  plot-only hook → reader fills in own psychology; biochemical response without inhabitation
  reader asks "how would I feel if that was me?" instead of "how would I feel if I was them?"
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] mirror neurons vs. identification + "me vs. them" test

---

GENRE LAYER (the contract)
  signal genre world within opening pages — reader must know what kind of story they're in
  establish global value at stake: life/death (thriller/action), love (romance), truth (mystery), justice (moral/legal), etc.
  world-building serves genre signaling; no world-building disconnected from wound-driven character behavior
  world-building timing rule: world earns its place only as the arena where surface desire is actively pursued

---

SYMPATHY LAYER (the invitation)
  "save the cat" mechanic — an active move that makes the reader root for this character
  distinct from relatability: internal conflict → identification; likable action → sympathy; both required
  a perfectly calibrated wound with no active sympathy move = reader won't follow
  target: reader wants them to succeed, not only understands why they're struggling

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  "especially bad" principle: any external opening event must be especially bad for THIS character
    → [[universal-diagnostics]] swappability test applied at hook
  delivery path selection: wound-first vs. mystery-first determines backstory placement
    → [[backstory-exposition]] for path selection rule and dramatized vs. summarized diagnostic

---

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  what is this character's surface desire?
  what dominant mechanism are they running — is it intact and well-practiced?
  does the opening signal genre and global value within the first pages?
  is there an active sympathy move, or only wound-based identification?
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] "me vs. them" test: can a reader inhabit this specific psychology?
  is any external event especially bad for this specific character, or just bad for anyone?
