# Inciting Incident — Act 1 Beat 2
# source: "How to Write an INCITING INCIDENT" — Abbie Emmons — https://youtu.be/JW9D_Zu-GDQ
# synthesis: [[wound-model-thesis]], [[wound-protective-mechanisms]], [[internal-conflict]], [[universal-diagnostics]]
# cross-references: [[Beats/01_hook]], [[Beats/03_build_up]]
# position: ~10–15% (first quarter; wound context from hook must be in place before this lands)

---

WOUND LAYER
state: dominant mechanism under active external threat for the first time; misbelief activates; wound-system engages as a whole
  abstract desire: still subterranean — NOT established here; first surfaces at aha moment
  COMFORT ZONE = MECHANISM'S DEFENDED TERRITORY
    the narrow band the dominant mechanism has actively maintained between two fear layers:
      inner boundary: raw wound (going deeper = wound directly re-triggered; internal fear)
      outer boundary: misbelief's predicted consequences (going further out = external fear realized)
    II = external event threatening the outer boundary for the first time in the story
    → [[internal-conflict]] TWO FEAR LAYERS; [[wound-protective-mechanisms]] dominant mechanism
  DESIRE CRYSTALLIZATION (Truby)
    before II: wound, misbelief, and habitual surface desire pattern — general and directionless
    after II: surface desire becomes a specific, concrete, directional story goal with a concrete object
    the II is what converts wound-driven habit into active story pursuit
  MECHANISM-SPECIFIC CONTACT
    avoider: the thing being avoided is now unavoidable — the route is closing
    overcompensator: performance of invulnerability directly and publicly challenged
    surrenderer: misbelief-as-refuge threatened; wrong comfort becomes unreliable
    → [[wound-protective-mechanisms]] scene-level activation

---

GENRE LAYER
state: genre contract activated; global value takes first swing (McKee)
  hook signaled genre world and global value at stake; II activates that contract for the first time
  global value swings — positive to negative or negative to positive — at this beat
  II must be a GENRE-APPROPRIATE EVENT: an event that belongs to this specific genre world
    wrong: an event that could incite any story in any genre
    right: an event that could only belong to this genre's value system
  STORY QUESTION ACTIVATION (Weiland)
    hook introduced character and wound; II is where the novel's SPECIFIC story question is raised
    not the genre question ("will love prevail?") but THIS story's version:
    "will THIS person risk X to get Y without losing Z?"
  WORLD-BUILDING ROLE: DELIVERY VEHICLE
    the II must grow causally from the story's world and its inhabitants — not arrive as convenience
    world-building earns its place here as the mechanism that makes the II feel inevitable
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] genre layer (world-building timing rule)

---

SYMPATHY LAYER
state: established sympathy tested for the first time
  hook secured sympathy through save-the-cat mechanic
  II = the first time something genuinely bad happens to someone the reader has been set up to root for
  WOUND-SPECIFIC CALIBRATION AS SYMPATHY MULTIPLIER
    generic bad event → reader feels general concern
    wound-calibrated II → "the worst possible thing for this specific person just happened"
    swappability test and sympathy multiplier are the same mechanism seen from two angles
  PITY TRAP
    purely passive character receiving the II → sympathy shades into pity (observer mode, not inhabitant)
    prevention: character must show SOME quality of response — fear, confusion, instinctive reflex
    any active quality keeps the reader inhabiting the character rather than watching from outside
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] sympathy layer (save-the-cat move now being tested)

---

RELATIONAL LAYER (the moral arc)
function: FIRST CONTACT — mechanism's relational distortion meets the story's central stakes
  the II disrupts the relational landscape established in the hook:
    introduces a new relationship the arc will run through, OR activates/threatens an existing one
  character's first mechanism-driven response to the II has an immediate relational consequence
    someone absorbs the first cost of the wound behavior — often before anyone in the scene names it
  reader can see the damage beginning; characters may not register it yet
  note: pity trap (sympathy layer) has a relational parallel
    passive character receiving the II has no relational behavior to show; first contact never happens
  → [[Beats/01_hook]] relational layer (relationship established there is now in first contact)

  CALIBRATION
    subtext principle: relational consequence embedded in the mechanism-driven response — not stated
    embed rule: relational moment lives within the scene's primary beat — not a standalone relationship scene
    weight: LIGHT — a single specific moment plants the pattern without announcing it
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] relational layer for universal calibration principles

  RELATIONAL FAILURE MODE: MELODRAMA
    over-dramatizing the first relational consequence before reader investment can hold it
    other characters reacting with disproportionate emotional force to early-stage wound behavior

---

FAILURE MODE: PLOT EVENT (no wound contact)
  II lands as external event without engaging the character's specific psychology
  reader fills their own psychology into the gap — mirror neurons, not identification
  causes: wrong II (swappable), wound not established in hook, or character too passive to show response
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] mirror neurons vs. identification; SURVIVAL MODE (hook-level parallel)

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  swappability test: II must threaten THIS mechanism's defended territory — not anyone's
  genre-level swappability: would this II belong to a different genre? → wrong II
  causality requirement: II must grow from the story's world and characters — not arrive arbitrarily
  hook←II dependency: wound context must be established before II lands; absent wound = plot event only
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] swappability test; [[Beats/01_hook]] wound layer

---

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  what has this character's dominant mechanism been defending that this event now threatens?
  is this II swappable — wound-level AND genre-level?
  does it activate the global value established in the hook? (McKee value shift check)
  what is this story's specific question — is it now implicitly raised? (Weiland)
  what has the surface desire crystallized into — does the character now have a directional goal? (Truby)
  does the character show some quality of response, or are they purely passive? (pity trap check)
  does the II grow causally from the story's world, or does it arrive arbitrarily?
  does the reader hold enough wound context from the hook to receive this as a story event?
