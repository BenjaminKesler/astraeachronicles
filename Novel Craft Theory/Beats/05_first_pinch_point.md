---

WOUND LAYER
state: misbelief unchallenged; protagonist in "while still avoiding fear" mode; mechanism intact; wound-safe route selected at FPP not yet tested; protagonist unaware of approaching collision

  READER-SIDE BEAT (core structural distinction)
    protagonist's wound state does not change at this beat — only the reader's does
    all prior beats shift both protagonist state and reader understanding simultaneously
    First PP shifts reader understanding only: accumulated information advantage receives its first concrete target
    this is what makes vicarious suspense the default delivery mode — reader's advantage is what the beat runs on
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] "while still avoiding fear" (the assumption First PP shows is insufficient)

  WOUND-CALIBRATED ANTAGONIST (core requirement)
    antagonistic force must target what the misbelief is protecting — not just the surface desire
    without calibration: antagonist is swappable; reader fills own psychology; mirror neurons not identification
    with calibration: reader recognizes the specific collision coming before protagonist does
    → [[universal-diagnostics]] swappability test

  MISBELIEF-AS-ANTAGONIST (structural option)
    antagonistic force need not be external — protagonist's own misbelief can fill the structural slot
    when no external antagonist: misbelief IS the looming force; surface and sub-surface layers collapse into one
    most useful in quiet/internal stories or for arc variety across a series
    → [[internal-conflict]] MISBELIEF AS ACTIVE REFUGE; [[wound-model-thesis]] Part 3

GENRE LAYER
state: genre is validating (consistency check), not constitutive — constitutive function ended at FPP
  antagonistic force must be genre-calibrated: threat belongs to this genre's world and value system
  no major global value charge shift at this beat — First PP is a suspense beat, not a structural value hinge
    minor charge tightening possible: stable-danger → imminent-danger (same pole, increased proximity)
  → [[Beats/01_hook]] genre layer (global value established); [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] genre layer (last constitutive beat)

---

SUSPENSE LAYER (the operative mechanism of this beat)

  EMERGENT VS. MANUFACTURED (core distinction)
    manufactured suspense: staged ominousness without wound-model support; author withholds information artificially; reader notices the machinery
    emergent suspense: byproduct of wound-model-correct prior beats; reader's accumulated information advantage given a concrete shape
    emergent suspense doesn't feel like a technique — it feels like consequence
    modern readers are calibrated to detect manufactured suspense; emergent is nearly invisible
    diagnostic: is this reader-held information being given a target, or author-withheld information being released for effect?

  THREE DELIVERY MODES (Abbie)
    FULL VICARIOUS: reader sees something protagonist cannot — antagonist behind closed doors, "meanwhile on the dark side"
      strongest emotional engagement; reader dopamine from information advantage; default recommendation
    SUBTLE CLUES: protagonist-visible but meaning-opaque; protagonist sees the clue, not what it signals
      reader connects what protagonist cannot; mild suspense; suits mystery-contract genres
    SHARED SUSPENSE: protagonist aware of threat; both reader and protagonist feel pressure
      weaker reader information advantage; more appropriate for Second PP when protagonist-awareness has grown
    → vicarious default for First PP; shared more fitting at Second PP (mechanism degraded; protagonist closer to aware)

  HITCHCOCK PRINCIPLE
    suspense > surprise; surprise = 15 seconds; suspense = 15 minutes
    whenever possible: inform the reader; reserve surprise for plot twists where the unexpected IS the highlight
    knowing what might happen does not make a story predictable — how it affects characters is what matters

SYMPATHY LAYER
state: dramatic irony accumulated across prior beats; First PP deploys it as active suspense for the first time

  reader information stack at this beat:
    wound model + mechanism at full strength (Hook)
    → crystallized desire + mechanism's defended territory first threatened (II)
    → fear architecture visible + "while still avoiding fear" seeded (Build-up)
    → irreversibility + full dramatic irony peak + "while still avoiding fear" named (FPP)
    → First PP: stack given its first concrete target
  reader now holds both what the protagonist hopes AND what threatens to end those hopes
  sympathy effect: aching-for deepens into dread; reader urgency to warn the protagonist peaks at this beat
  → [[Beats/03_build_up]] sympathy layer; [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] sympathy layer (dramatic irony maximum in Act 1)

---

RELATIONAL LAYER
function: IMPLIED THREAT — reader sees what the looming antagonist will eventually cost the relationships recruited into the wound-safe plan

  protagonist has recruited relationships into the wound-safe plan at and after FPP
  reader can see the antagonist's looming will eventually exact a relational cost — protagonist cannot
  no named relational consequence yet; reader holds the implication
  → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] relational layer (commitment cost locked in there; implied threat is its downstream signal)

  CALIBRATION
    weight: very light; subtext only; no dedicated relational scene
    embed rule: relational implication felt within the beat — never the scene's primary purpose
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] relational layer for universal calibration principles

---

FAILURE MODES
  MANUFACTURED SUSPENSE: staged ominousness without prior wound-model work; reader detects the technique; identification breaks
  SWAPPABLE ANTAGONIST: threat doesn't connect to the misbelief's protected territory; any protagonist would feel this equally; mirror neurons only
  DEMONSTRATED TOO EARLY: antagonist shown at full capability; depletes Act 2 escalation; loom is the correct intensity for this beat
    demonstrated capability belongs to the internal Act 2 pinches and Second PP, not here

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  wound-calibrated antagonist: non-negotiable; threat must connect to what the misbelief is protecting
  delivery mode selection: vicarious default; subtle clues when mystery is the genre contract; shared only when protagonist-awareness serves the story
  loom not demonstrate: antagonist implies threat from distance; full demonstration reserved for internal Act 2 pinches onward
  stakes activation: reader's first sight of what makes the stakes concrete — not refresh (too early to forget); activation
  reader-side beat check: protagonist's wound state should be unchanged after this beat; if it shifts here, the beat is doing Act 2 work prematurely

  ACT 2 PINCH ARCHITECTURE (four-grouping framework)
    First PP (this beat): structural anchor; ominous transition; reader information advantage given first concrete target; loom only
    internal Act 2A pinches: not named beats; embedded in the Reactionary Hero phase; each closes one wound-avoidance path; escalate demonstrated capability progressively
    internal Act 2B pinches: same pattern with protagonist's mechanism visibly degrading; antagonist closer; shared suspense begins to enter
    Second PP: structural anchor; antagonist near-maximum proximity; mechanism degraded; shared suspense fully viable
    → [[Beats/06_pre_midpoint_reactionary_hero]] for full Reactionary Hero phase mechanics
    → [[Beats/09_second_pinch_point]]

  POSITION INHERITANCE
    from FPP: "while still avoiding fear" active; protagonist committed; door closed (unaware); wound-safe route selected — this route is the antagonist's calibration target at this beat
    from Build-up: fear architecture established; retreat option seeded mistaken belief in reversibility
    from II: crystallized desire as the specific goal the antagonist now threatens
    from Hook: wound model fully in place; mechanism at full strength — the reader's information advantage that makes this beat work begins here

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  is the antagonistic force wound-calibrated — does it target what the misbelief is protecting, or only the surface desire?
  is the suspense emergent (built from prior beats) or manufactured (staged for effect)?
  which delivery mode — vicarious, subtle clues, or shared suspense? Does it serve this story's genre contract?
  is the antagonist looming or demonstrating capability? (loom correct here; full demonstration premature)
  does the protagonist's wound state remain unchanged — or is this beat doing Act 2 work prematurely?
  can the reader connect the antagonist's looming to the wound-safe route selected at the FPP?
  does this concretize the stakes — can the reader now picture what failure looks like?
