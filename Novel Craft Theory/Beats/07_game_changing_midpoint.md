---

WOUND LAYER
state: misbelief disrupted at plan level; wound-safe route selected at FPP terminated; protagonist surprised; goal shift forced; misbelief intensifies (does not weaken); overcompensation peaks; protagonist unaware of where new territory leads

  MISBELIEF-SURPRISE (core mechanism)
    protagonist is surprised because the wound's own plan failed on its own terms — not merely external interference
    misbelief told them the plan would work; the midpoint shows it cannot, in a way they could not route around
    distinct from generic plot surprise: the surprise is calibrated to the specific logic of the misbelief
    without this calibration: GCM is swappable — any protagonist would respond equally → EXTERNAL TWIST failure mode

  PROTAGONIST-SIDE BEAT (structural contrast)
    first Act 2 beat where protagonist state actually shifts
    all prior Act 2 beats (First PP, pre-midpoint phase) advanced reader information while protagonist state remained unchanged
    GCM is the inverse of the First PP: First PP = reader gains information, protagonist state static; GCM = protagonist state shifts for the first time
    → [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]] reader-side beat (contrast)

  MISBELIEF INTENSIFICATION (post-GCM response)
    protagonist's response to plan disruption is deeper commitment to misbelief, not weakening
    misbelief functions as active refuge — most threatened = runs toward it hardest
    protagonist most certain of the wrong belief precisely when wound is most threatened
    false victory variant: maximum misbelief commitment — protagonist believes they've proven the misbelief true
    this is why the post-midpoint protagonist becomes proactive: urgency driven by intensified misbelief, not by new truth
    → [[wound-model-thesis]] Part 2 (misbelief as active refuge); [[wound-protective-mechanisms]] overcompensation

  DOORWAY OF NO RETURN #2 (Bell)
    two distinct irreversibility points in the arc; this is the second
    Doorway #1 (FPP): door to ordinary world closed; protagonist unaware; hidden structural fact
    Doorway #2 (GCM): door to original wound-safe route closed; protagonist more aware game has changed; blind to where new territory leads
    protagonist can't un-commit from the new collision course; can't return to the specific strategy selected at FPP
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] irreversibility (Doorway #1 and forward pointer)

  abstract desire: still subterranean; GCM does not approach aha moment territory

---

GENRE LAYER
state: genre validating (consistency check), not constitutive — constitutive function ended at FPP

  GCM VS. TWIST ENDING (structural distinction)
    all GCMs are plot twists; not all plot twists are GCMs
    GCM: midpoint-positioned; character-facing; meaningful impact on protagonist going forward; story-engine function
    twist ending (Twilight Zone): ending-positioned; audience-facing; premise fulfillment; pleasure from retroactive re-read
    position + direction of impact are what distinguish them; a twist that doesn't shift protagonist state is not a GCM regardless of timing

  genre determines native charge polarity (see CHARGE POLARITY below) and re-key depth and track
  conspiracy thriller native form: false victory — coded reality promise primes reader for active clue-scanning from hook forward

---

CHARGE POLARITY (Snyder)
  GCM event arrives as FALSE VICTORY or FALSE DEFEAT — design variable, not structural requirement
  both terminate the wound-safe route; through opposite charge polarities; different emotional textures; different retroactive re-keys

  FALSE DEFEAT
    plan fails unexpectedly; protagonist blindsided; goal shift crisis-driven
    misbelief response: "that angle was wrong; the misbelief isn't — find a different route"
    reader experience: dread + urgency; protagonist in danger doubling down on wrong solution
    pity trap risk: protagonist may look like punching bag if plan wasn't genuinely owned → see FAILURE MODES

  FALSE VICTORY
    plan appears to succeed; surface desire apparently satisfied; protagonist doesn't feel the vacancy yet
    goal shifts from "get the thing" to "protect/extend the victory"
    misbelief response: "I've proven it works — press the advantage"
    produces MAXIMUM MISBELIEF COMMITMENT: protagonist most wrong when most confident
    reader experience: tragic irony; protagonist's conviction IS the reader's dread
    distinct from SUPPOSED VICTORY beat (pre-disaster): different position, different function; do not conflate

  SERIES / NOVEL HIERARCHY
    series genre agreement establishes native polarity; Novel 1 must match series agreement (establishes the reader contract before variation is attempted)
    subsequent novels may use non-native polarity for craft variety
    variation must remain genre-inflected: in conspiracy thriller, even a false defeat wears conspiracy clothes — the defeat may be manufactured, orchestrated, or a move in someone else's game

---

VALUE CHARGE SHIFT (McKee)
  GCM produces the third major global value charge shift
    II: first swing — value put in play
    FPP: second swing — irreversible commitment
    GCM: third swing — direction determined by polarity
      false defeat → negative charge shift (protagonist's position worsens)
      false victory → positive charge shift (protagonist's position apparently improves; reader sees the falseness)
  without this shift: GCM can be mechanically correct but not felt as a structural beat by the reader
  → [[Beats/02_inciting_incident]], [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] value charge chain

---

SYMPATHY LAYER
state: reader information stack at maximum; dramatic irony carries tragic quality for the first time

  reader holds: full wound model + misbelief's fatal logic + the false nature of victory or defeat + Doorway #2 closure protagonist doesn't fully see
  false defeat: dread + urgency; reader watches protagonist in danger doubling down wrong
  false victory: tragic irony; protagonist's confidence is the reader's dread; protagonist most wrong when most certain
  → [[Beats/06_pre_midpoint_reactionary_hero]] sympathy layer (full stack deployed through pre-midpoint; GCM is where it first carries tragic weight)

---

RELATIONAL LAYER
function: FIRST VISIBLE COST — relational damage loading since FPP first becomes concretely visible

  commitment cost locked in at FPP; loading through pre-midpoint micro-cycles; by GCM first showing as specific damage in specific relationships
  false defeat: plan failure puts visible stress on relationships recruited into the plan; regrouping may impose additional relational cost
  false victory: apparent success requires protagonist to deepen wound-driven relational patterns; cost of winning begins to surface
  still reader-held; characters beginning to feel something without naming it
  → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] relational layer (commitment cost locked in there); [[Beats/06_pre_midpoint_reactionary_hero]] relational layer (resource test loading)

  CALIBRATION
    weight: MODERATELY PRESENT — more visible than pre-midpoint; pattern surfacing but not articulated
    subtext principle: damage concretely visible to reader before named by any character
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] relational layer for universal calibration principles

---

RETROACTIVE RE-EXAMINATION
  pleasure of GCM comes not only from the surprise but from looking back at pre-midpoint material in its light
  well-constructed earlier clues "take on new satisfying significance" — cognitive re-contextualization, not emotional anchoring
  distinct from planted resonance ([[scene-earning]]): planted resonance = emotional anchors at payoff; retroactive re-key = reader re-reads prior events with new understanding of what they actually meant

  TWO-TRACK RE-KEY (genre-dependent)
    world-level track: clues about what was really happening in the plot — who was pulling strings, what information actually meant; genre contract fulfillment; does not deplete character-level track
    character-level track: clues about how the misbelief was driving behavior; strategy visible in retrospect; must stop short of wound revelation
    conspiracy thriller: native two-track capacity; world mystery can be unlocked at GCM; character mystery protected for aha moment

  RE-KEY DEPTH = GCM DEPTH
    plan-level GCM: re-keys Act 2A only
    misbelief-level GCM: can reach Act 1; seeding obligation extends as far back as the re-key is designed to reach
    retroactive look cannot go deeper into the internal character than the GCM itself goes

  AHA MOMENT PROTECTION (non-negotiable)
    retroactive re-key illuminates HOW (the strategy was misbelief-driven) — not WHAT (what the character actually needed)
    abstract desire must remain subterranean; full legibility belongs to the aha moment exclusively
    → [[Beats/06_pre_midpoint_reactionary_hero]] seeding obligation (design requirement there)

---

FAILURE MODES
  EXTERNAL TWIST: GCM lands as plot reversal without engaging protagonist's specific wound; "just a thing happening"; any protagonist would respond equally; internal conflict bypassed → mirror neurons, not identification
  PUNCHING BAG: protagonist purely receives the midpoint event; no owned wound-safe plan to terminate; goal shift has nowhere to come from; protagonist continues to be acted upon at story's second major hinge
  READER-ONLY SURPRISE: event surprises reader but not protagonist; protagonist state unchanged; beat functions as reader-side event like First PP; Act 2B has no new wound-driven engine
  AHA MOMENT THEFT: retroactive re-key goes too deep into internal character; abstract desire becomes legible at GCM; aha moment depleted before it arrives
  PITY TRAP (false defeat variant): false defeat delivered externally; protagonist didn't own the plan being terminated; punching bag dynamic re-emerges; pity replaces identification
  → [[Beats/02_inciting_incident]] PLOT EVENT failure mode (parallel at II); [[Beats/06_pre_midpoint_reactionary_hero]] PUNCHING BAG re-emergence

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  misbelief-surprise calibration: GCM event must target the misbelief's own logic — what the wound's plan was protecting; not just external reversal
  charge polarity selection: false victory or false defeat; genre + series agreement primary; novel-level variation permitted after agreement established; variation must be genre-inflected
  re-key depth matched to seeding: retroactive look only reaches as far back as clues were planted; seeding obligation begins at GCM design, not execution
  aha moment protection: re-key reveals HOW (strategy), not WHAT (abstract desire); strictly enforced
  doorway #2 earned: protagonist must have owned the wound-safe route being terminated; borrowed plan produces no genuine irreversibility
  scale independence: wound-engagement is the criterion, not external magnitude; intimate revelation can outperform an epic event if better wound-calibrated
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] swappability test; character lens audit

  POSITION INHERITANCE
    from Pre-Midpoint Reactionary Hero: phase-end exhaustion = transition mechanism; GCM is the wall the reactive mode runs into; micro-cycles have closed sub-routes; GCM closes the main route; pre-midpoint scenes seeded for retroactive re-key
    from First PP: wound-calibrated antagonist now at closer range; Act 2A internal pinches have progressively closed sub-routes; reader information advantage at maximum heading into protagonist-state shift
    from FPP: wound-safe route selected there terminates here; "while still avoiding fear" assumption disrupted at plan level; values revelation still opaque; Doorway #1 preceded this
    from Build-up: fear architecture established; "while still avoiding fear" seeded; protagonist operated from that assumption through pre-midpoint phase
    from II: crystallized desire = specific goal now being shifted
    from Hook: retroactive re-key reaches here if seeded; abstract desire incongruity leak (Hauge) = deepest possible anchor
    what this beat opens: post-midpoint action hero phase; protagonist proactive at higher urgency; misbelief-steered new plan at overcompensation peak; antagonist closer; Second PP approaching

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  what is the misbelief's own logic that the GCM disrupts — is the protagonist surprised because the wound's plan failed, or only because something external interfered? (misbelief-surprise check)
  is this a false victory or false defeat — does the charge polarity match the genre agreement and series contract?
  does the protagonist's goal visibly shift — is there a new wound-safe angle they now commit to?
  did the protagonist own the plan being terminated, or did the plot hand them a plan? (Doorway #2 earned check)
  what does the retroactive re-key illuminate — HOW the strategy was misbelief-driven (correct) or WHAT the character actually needed (aha moment theft risk)?
  how far back does the re-key reach — and was the seeding designed to support that depth?
  what global value has shifted — which direction, and can the reader feel it without being told? (value charge check)
  what relational cost is first becoming concretely visible? (first visible cost check)
  does the protagonist's post-midpoint doubling down look rational from inside the misbelief? (character lens audit)
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] swappability test; character lens audit; mirror neurons vs. identification
