# position: ~62–75% (Act 2B; between Post-Midpoint and Disaster; most sources place near the 3/4 mark)

---

WOUND LAYER
state: protagonist feels first enclosure; overcompensation responds with harder doubling down; first mechanism strain visible to reader; antagonist at maximum proximity before disaster; self-causer pattern readable by reader; misbelief still operative

  READER-SIDE + PROTAGONIST-SIDE HYBRID (structural distinction from First PP)
    First PP: reader-side beat only — protagonist state unchanged; only reader's information advanced
    Second PP: hybrid — protagonist begins to FEEL the enclosure for the first time
    protagonist does not have full awareness; felt enclosure is behavioral pressure, not revelation
    → [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]] reader-side beat (contrast)

  FELT ENCLOSURE (Weiland — new at this beat)
    protagonist subjectively registers diminishing options for the first time
    response: harder overcompensation — protagonist doubles down, works harder, pushes further
    felt enclosure + harder doubling down = first visible mechanism strain from the reader's POV
    protagonist interprets the enclosure as "I need to try harder" — the misbelief's response to threat
    this is not awareness, not revelation — the misbelief is still running; strain is the mechanism under load
    → [[wound-protective-mechanisms]] arc diagnostic (late: visibly strained; cracks visible to reader before character feels them)

  SELF-CAUSER SEEDING FUNCTION (core mechanism of this beat)
    antagonist has "gained considerable ground since Act 1" — this gain is not independent
    partially made of the protagonist's own competent-false-framed choices accumulating since the FPP
    reader at the Second PP can trace this causal chain backward; protagonist cannot
    the self-causer pattern becomes readable here — not yet the full disaster reveal, but the pattern is legible
    CRITICAL FRAMING: antagonist gained ground through protagonist's competent choices through a false frame — NOT through incompetence
      reader response: dread ("I see exactly how they got here") not frustration ("why did they do that?")
      identification holds because the chain is traceable, not punishing
    → [[plot-structure-outlining]] SELF-CAUSER MECHANISM (disaster-level; Second PP is its first readable surfacing)
    → [[Beats/08_post_midpoint_action_hero]] seeding obligation (Act 2B choices are what this gain is built on)

  WOUND-CALIBRATED ANTAGONIST (carries from First PP — same requirement, closer range)
    threat must connect to what the misbelief is protecting — not just the surface plan
    antagonist can be external force OR protagonist's own misbelief "always looming in the background, getting ready to attack them come Act 3"
    at Second PP: same calibration, closer proximity, more concrete threat shape
    → [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]] wound-calibrated antagonist (origin)

  abstract desire: still subterranean; no approach toward aha moment territory

GENRE LAYER
state: genre validating (consistency check), not constitutive; antagonist proximity must be genre-calibrated
  minor value charge shift possible: stable-danger → imminent-danger (same negative pole, increased proximity)
  no major structural charge shift — that belongs to the disaster
  conspiracy thriller: Second PP may be where complexity expansion AND threat proximity converge in the same beat
    protagonist receives information that expands the conspiracy map while showing the antagonist closer than expected
  → [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]] genre layer (last constitutive beat was FPP; same here)

---

SUSPENSE LAYER (operative mechanism — inherited from First PP with distinctions)

  EMERGENT VS. MANUFACTURED (inherited — same requirement)
    Second PP suspense must be emergent: reader's accumulated wound-model information + self-causer chain = the threat
    manufactured suspense breaks identification: staged ominousness without wound-model support
    → [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]] emergent vs. manufactured (origin)

  DELIVERY MODE (more flexible than First PP)
    VICARIOUS: reader sees what protagonist cannot — antagonist's POV, "meanwhile on the dark side"
    SHARED: protagonist sees a clue, doesn't register its significance; reader connects what protagonist cannot
    HYBRID: protagonist beginning to feel pressure (felt enclosure) while reader sees the full picture
    shared suspense more viable here than at First PP: protagonist is beginning to feel the walls closing
    no single correct method — creative freedom on how revealing or cryptic; match to protagonist awareness level and genre contract
    → [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]] three delivery modes (vicarious default there; full flexibility here)

---

SYMPATHY LAYER
state: reader information stack complete; reader can trace the self-causer chain the protagonist cannot; tragic dread at maximum before disaster

  reader holds: full wound model + protagonist's competent-false-framed choices + causal chain to antagonist's position + protagonist's felt enclosure without its full significance
  effect: dread not frustration — reader sees exactly how the protagonist arrived here through reasonable choices
  protagonist's harder doubling-down in response to felt enclosure = mechanism strain visible before protagonist feels the crack
  → [[Beats/08_post_midpoint_action_hero]] sympathy layer (two-tension-track at maximum entering here)

---

RELATIONAL LAYER
function: BOND UNDER PRESSURE — antagonist's proximity and protagonist's wound-driven responses specifically pressure the relational bonds

  antagonist's gained ground targets what holds the team together — not just the surface plan; relational bonds at specific risk
  THREE DESIGN OPTIONS (not mutually exclusive; choose based on story and genre):
    (1) EXTERNAL PRESSURE: antagonist specifically targets the relational bonds; team threatened as a unit
    (2) MECHANISM MISMATCH FRICTION: protagonists under maximum stress revert to dominant mechanisms; mismatches generate friction without fracture
    (3) WOUND-DRIVEN MISTAKE: one protagonist's wound drives a choice that inadvertently harms the group; reckoning with it advances the relational arc
  found family context: external pressure + mechanism friction honors the bond test without requiring internal betrayal
    the choice to stay together despite friction IS the arc progressing
  forward: relational cost loading here becomes visible crisis at the disaster
  → [[Beats/08_post_midpoint_action_hero]] relational layer (deepening cost → bond under pressure here)
  → [[wound-protective-mechanisms]] SCENE FRICTION (mechanism mismatch between characters — tool for option 2)

---

FAILURE MODES
  MANUFACTURED SUSPENSE: staged ominousness without prior wound-model work; reader detects the technique; emergent suspense broken
  SWAPPABLE ANTAGONIST: threat doesn't connect to misbelief-protected territory; any protagonist would feel this equally; mirror neurons not identification
  DEMONSTRATED TOO EARLY: antagonist at full capability; depletes disaster escalation; loom is the correct intensity at this beat
  INCOMPETENCE FRAMING: antagonist's gained ground attributed to protagonist's errors not competent-false-framed choices; reader frustrated not dreading; identification breaks
  PITY TRAP RE-EMERGENCE: protagonist made passive or overwhelmed by antagonist's advance; visible agency required — protagonist must respond to felt enclosure with active doubling down
  FULL FRACTURE (found family): relational pressure escalates to internal betrayal before the disaster has earned it; bond undermined rather than tested

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  emergent suspense: antagonist's proximity traceable backward through protagonist's prior choices; reader can make the self-causer connection; pattern legible here, fully revealed at disaster
  wound-calibrated antagonist: non-negotiable; same calibration as First PP at closer range
  felt enclosure: protagonist shows first behavioral response to pressure — harder doubling down, visible effort increase; not full awareness; mechanism strain beginning to surface
  loom not demonstrate: near-maximum proximity; full capability demonstration belongs to disaster
  delivery mode selection: vicarious, shared, or hybrid — no single correct method; match to protagonist awareness level
  relational design: one of three options or combination; found family context favors external pressure or mechanism mismatch over internal betrayal

  POSITION INHERITANCE
    from Post-Midpoint Action Hero: protagonist's competent-false-framed choices in Act 2B are what antagonist's gained ground is partially built on; two-tension-track at maximum entering here; progressive complexity has expanded the problem map; relational bonds being tested under increasing pressure; protagonist at overcompensation peak entering felt enclosure
    from Game-Changing Midpoint: Doorway #2 committed; protagonist on new wound-safe angle; first visible cost = relational starting point
    from First Pinch Point: wound-calibrated antagonist established there; delivery mode contrast (vicarious default there; flexibility here); loom principle carries forward unchanged
    from FPP: wound-safe route (updated at GCM) still executing; "while still avoiding fear" still operative; commitment cost compounding
    what this beat opens: Disaster — mechanism breaks; self-causer pattern becomes fully visible and unavoidable; protagonist's wound directly exposed; relational cost loading since FPP becomes explicit crisis
      → [[plot-structure-outlining]] SELF-CAUSER MECHANISM, KRYPTONITE, DARK MOMENT

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  is the antagonist's gained ground emergent — traceable backward through protagonist's prior competent-false-framed choices? (self-causer seeding check)
  does the antagonist's threat connect to what the misbelief is protecting — not just the surface plan? (wound-calibrated check)
  is the protagonist showing felt enclosure through behavioral response (harder doubling down) — not full awareness or revelation? (felt enclosure check)
  which delivery mode fits — vicarious, shared, or hybrid? Does protagonist awareness level at this beat support shared suspense? (delivery mode check)
  does the relational pressure test the bond without fracturing it prematurely — external targeting, mechanism friction, or wound-driven mistake? (found family / relational design check)
  is the antagonist looming at near-maximum proximity — not demonstrating full capability? (loom check)
  can a reader trace how protagonist's competent-false-framed choices contributed to antagonist's position — without making the protagonist look incompetent? (self-causer framing check)
  is the protagonist showing visible agency in response to the threat — doubling down, not absorbing? (pity trap check)
