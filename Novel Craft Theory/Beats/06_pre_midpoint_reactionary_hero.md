# position: ~25–50% (Act 2A; First Plot Point through Midpoint; longest single phase in the arc)

---

WOUND LAYER
state: misbelief fully operational; every decision traceable to its logic; "while still avoiding fear" as operative mode; mechanism intact but progressively strained; wound-safe route executing; protagonist unaware of approaching termination

  THE STUPID PLAN (core diagnostic)
    misbelief made operational as a strategy
    coherent from inside the misbelief; doomed from outside it
    protagonist genuinely believes this plan delivers both desire and wound-avoidance
    reader can see the doom; protagonist cannot — this gap is the phase's primary tension engine
    → [[wound-model-thesis]] Part 3 (surface desire as fear-avoidance wearing a costume)

  MISBELIEF-AS-DECISION-ENGINE (non-negotiable requirement)
    every protagonist decision in this phase must be traceable to their specific misbelief
    swappability test: if other characters would pursue the same plan for the same reasons, the misbelief isn't driving it
    Divergent diagnostic: Tris trains hard so she doesn't end up factionless — so does every other initiate; plan not wound-driven
    Greatest Showman diagnostic: every Barnum decision routes back to "you are not successful unless admired by everyone"
    → [[universal-diagnostics]] swappability test

  MICRO-CYCLE ARCHITECTURE (the phase's internal structure)
    protagonist doesn't know every step of the plan — structural permission, not characterization weakness
    each cycle: McKee gap (expectation vs. outcome) → Weiland R→R→R (reaction → reflection → response) → wound-driven decision
    McKee gap: protagonist acts, expects a result, gets a different result; gap opens; drives the next step
    Weiland interior rhythm: event hits (emotional reaction) → protagonist processes (rational reflection) → protagonist decides (wound-driven response)
    each cycle closes one wound-avoidance sub-route; pressure compounds
    one decision leads to the next in natural cause-and-effect choreography — not random events
    → [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]] Act 2 Pinch Architecture (internal Act 2A pinches named there)

  ESCALATION REQUIREMENT (Bell)
    cycles must escalate, not repeat; each turn must be worse than the last
    mechanism: each cycle closes a sub-route → fewer options → higher stakes on remaining options
    flat knockbacks = no compression; story stalls; reader loses forward pull

  PHASE-END EXHAUSTION (transition mechanism)
    late scenes push the reactive mode to its limit
    protagonist working hardest precisely as sub-routes narrow
    sets up midpoint as a wall the reactive mode runs into — not an interruption from outside
    → [[wound-protective-mechanisms]] overcompensation (post-midpoint: misbelief intensifies before eventual collapse)

---

GENRE LAYER
state: genre validating (consistency check), not structurally constitutive; genre experience delivered at full intensity — these are different functions
  Snyder "Promise of the Premise": this phase delivers the genre experience the reader came for
    thriller: thrills; romance: romantic tension; mystery: puzzle deepening
    genre-delivery is a reader-consumption function, not a structural mechanism; constitutive work ended at FPP
    distinction: genre stops defining what events mean (constitutive) but the genre experience runs at full force (delivery)
    design risk: wound-model mechanics crowding out genre satisfaction; both must be present
  global value charge: active but not undergoing major structural shift in this phase; minor turns at each micro-cycle (McKee); major shift reserved for midpoint
  → [[Beats/01_hook]] genre layer (global value established); [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] genre layer (last constitutive beat)

---

SYMPATHY LAYER
state: dramatic irony from FPP fully deployed; reader information advantage with concrete target from First PP; reader watches protagonist execute a pre-loaded failure with conviction

  reader information stack at this phase:
    wound model + mechanism at full strength (Hook)
    → crystallized desire as the specific goal being pursued (II)
    → fear architecture + "while still avoiding fear" assumption (Build-up)
    → irreversibility gap + structural commitment (FPP)
    → antagonist's wound-calibrated target (First PP)
    → this phase: full stack deployed in real time against the stupid plan

  dramatic irony: now ACTIVE, not accumulating — reader uses their advantage on every micro-cycle
  sympathy state: "aching for" deepens into dread-plus-forward-pull
    reader wants protagonist to succeed; knows this route can't deliver it; can't look away
  → [[Beats/03_build_up]] sympathy layer ("aching for" established there); [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] sympathy layer (dramatic irony at maximum)

  PITY TRAP RE-EMERGENCE
    reactive orientation can make protagonist look passive; pity replaces identification
    prevention: protagonist must be visibly executing a plan even while responding to obstacles
    agency must remain on the page — even reactive agency is agency
    → [[Beats/02_inciting_incident]] pity trap (original; same mechanism re-emerging at Act 2 scale)

---

RELATIONAL LAYER
function: RESOURCE TEST — relationships recruited into the wound-safe plan are now being tested; new relationships form as resources; accumulated cost loading quietly

  Vogler (Tests/Allies/Enemies): protagonist acquires relational resources, tests existing relationships, identifies opposition
    relational toolkit for Act 2B assembled here
    distinct from wound-driven relational distortion tracking — this is the operational layer
  accumulated cost: each micro-cycle that closes a wound-avoidance sub-route also loads additional cost onto wound-driven relational patterns
    reader sees the toll compounding; characters cannot yet name it
  forward: relational cost loading here becomes visible at Second PP and explicit at the disaster
  → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] relational layer (commitment cost locked in there; now being tested)

  CALIBRATION
    weight: MODERATE — more present than FPP; pattern compounding but still primarily subtext
    no dedicated confrontation scenes; relational damage embedded within plan-execution scenes
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] relational layer for universal calibration principles

---

FAILURE MODES
  SWAPPABLE PLAN: plan not traceable to protagonist's specific misbelief; any character could pursue the same plan for the same reasons; story becomes plot-driven; reader fills own psychology; identification collapses
  PUNCHING BAG (re-emergence): micro-cycles feel like events happening to the protagonist, not the protagonist executing a plan; agency disappears; pity replaces identification
  FLAT KNOCKBACKS: cycles repeat without escalating; pressure doesn't compound; story stalls
  REACTIVE AS PASSIVE: misreading "reactionary" as passive; protagonist is reactive in orientation but must be actively executing a plan; energy must be on the page

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  misbelief-as-decision-engine: every decision traceable to the protagonist's specific misbelief — non-negotiable
  plan incompleteness: protagonist may not know every step; each step discovered through action; build cause-and-effect, not outline execution
  micro-cycle architecture: McKee gap structure + Weiland R→R→R interior rhythm; not just "obstacle → protagonist responds"
  escalation: each cycle must close a sub-route and raise the stakes; flat repetition is a failure mode
  phase-end exhaustion: late scenes push the reactive mode to its limit; protagonist working hardest as options narrow
  genre delivery: wound-model mechanics and genre satisfaction must coexist; don't let one crowd out the other
  seeding obligation: pre-midpoint scenes carry double-coded meaning the GCM will retroactively re-key; design each scene not only for forward plan execution but for what it will mean when re-read after the midpoint; re-key depth = GCM depth; conspiracy thriller: two-track seeding (world-level + character-level)
    → [[Beats/07_game_changing_midpoint]] retroactive re-examination

  POSITION INHERITANCE
    from First PP: antagonist wound-calibrated and targeting the wound-safe route; reader information advantage with concrete target; Act 2A internal pinch architecture active
    from FPP: wound-safe route = the specific plan now executing; "while still avoiding fear" as operative mode; irreversibility gap (protagonist still believes retreat is possible); commitment cost = relational resources now being tested; values revelation still subterranean
    from Build-up: fear architecture visible to reader; protagonist's retreat-viability illusion still active
    from II: crystallized desire = the specific goal the stupid plan is pursuing
    from Hook: mechanism at full strength at phase entry; relational landscape established; genre/value context
    what this phase opens: midpoint terminates the wound-safe route selected at FPP; post-midpoint protagonist doubles down at higher urgency (overcompensation at peak) before eventual mechanism collapse
      → [[Beats/07_game_changing_midpoint]] for midpoint mechanics, charge polarity, and retroactive re-examination

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  can every protagonist decision in this phase be traced to the specific misbelief — swappability test applied at each major decision?
  does the protagonist have a plan, even an incomplete one — or are they purely reacting to events? (agency check)
  does each micro-cycle follow McKee gap structure (expectation vs. outcome) and Weiland R→R→R interior rhythm?
  are cycles escalating — each one closing a sub-route and increasing pressure — or flat repetitions? (escalation check)
  is the protagonist visibly executing a plan even within the reactive orientation? (pity trap re-emergence check)
  are the late scenes pushing the plan to maximum effort as options narrow? (phase-end exhaustion check)
  is the genre experience being delivered at full intensity alongside the wound-model mechanics?
  what specific wound-safe route is being executed — is it concrete enough that its midpoint termination will land as a wall?
