# TYPE 4 — HEART · identity-as-wound

WOUND: can't beat inadequacy → learn to love it ("and that's beautiful"). The masochism is the defining move. Spin pain into dark tragic story (lore) → stop having the wound and become it.
KEY DISTINCTION FROM 9: four ≠ identity-searcher. Fours know EXACTLY who they are and are completely obsessed with that identity. The identity just happens to be built on pain, deficiency, and difference. Nine doesn't know who they are; four knows and is combative about it. Tyrion: "Wear it like armor." Not wondering — seized.
MECH [absent object]: have it → devalue it. Don't have it → idealize it. Present = ordinary; absent = luminous. Getting the thing doesn't fix it — the present becomes present, which means it immediately becomes ordinary.
MECH [narrativizing pain]: lore relieves obligation to actually change. "It's all for the plot." Four is invested in their pain, not just in pain — dramatically far more complex.
ARC: stop narrativizing the wound and become someone who is actually okay. "Becoming okay" = indistinguishable from death to the four. To cure the pain = lose the story = lose the self. Arc demands identity dissolution, not change. Victory looks like a loss from the inside.
ENGINE: four is not a character who wants to be saved — must be pried away from their own story. Won't come willingly. Attached to the darkness the way a three is attached to the persona.
TENSION: identity as armor = identity as prison. Any genuine healing threatens identity; any uncomplicated joy threatens identity; ordinary happiness must be rejected.
MECH [envy ≠ jealousy]: jealousy fears losing what you have. Envy desires what someone else has. Four envies the ease, the ordinary belonging, the simple pleasure of being fine — NOT the person who has it. Double bind: they'd refuse ordinariness if offered. They envy what they would reject.
STRESS→2: aloof independence → sudden neediness, clinging, desperate search for a savior. Disorienting to witness because it contradicts established affect.
INT→1: discipline, equanimity, channeling depth into principled action; learning to function and show up

SHAPES
  redeem: victory looks like a loss from the inside. Max returns to the "old mundane life" — win is that a bad thing is just bad, not lore. Lady Bird introduces herself as Christine — accepting the ordinary name = most radical act of the film.
  tragedy: black hole becomes North Star. Joker needs the world to be as dark as he is — if the world can be good and simple, the identity collapses. Works to make the world match. Succeeds.
  ULTIMATE (rarely depicted): gives up the story entirely — not replaced with a healthier special identity. Stands for a period as nobody. Stops being protagonist of own internal drama. Has never been cleanly depicted because it offers no satisfying identity endpoint.

ANTAG: nihilism evangelist. Doesn't just live in darkness — preaches it. Weapon = ideology (convincing others nothing is worth saving). Unique: may use the word "villain" about themselves as a point of pride — most self-aware antagonist posture and most self-deceived simultaneously.
Weakness: genuine unconditional beauty that cannot be narrativized away (Cindy Lou Who). If goodness exists and is real, the whole cosmology collapses.

NOT
  → NOT a passive sufferer — masochism is active: curates, narrativizes, protects darkness from contamination by joy, actively rejects anything that would simplify the story
  → darkness ≠ fourness — any type can be dark/emotional/artistic. Fourness = the "and that's beautiful" relationship to inadequacy specifically
  → does NOT have to be artistic — soldier, scientist, businessman can be a four if inner architecture = "I am defined by my wound"
  → the relational hunger is real and often missed: aloof presentation, heart type underneath; desperate to be seen for the actual broken interior; "will bring a camera to the woods"

DX
  1. What is the specific identity built around the wound — what story do they tell, what loss/deficiency/difference is at the center?
  2. What does the masochism look like in action — how do they actively curate/protect/display darkness rather than simply endure it?
  3. What is the absent object — what do others seem to have that this four cannot access? What's the paradox (they wouldn't actually want to become those people)?
  4. Where does the darkness actively serve them — what does keeping the wound give them (identity, community, creative material, exemption from having to change)?
  5. What would they lose if they got better — if the pain went away, what would remain of the self?
  6. What relationship in the story refuses to be dramatic — offers steady, ordinary, un-narrativizable love?
  7. Is this four an active curator or a more private carrier — performing for an audience, or suppressing beneath a functional exterior?
  8. Who is the witness — the person who knows the story and reflects it back? What happens if that witness is removed?
  9. When does this four move toward two under stress — where does aloof independence collapse into clinging and desperate need for a savior?
  10. What does healing look like specifically — what would have to die in them? Can they tolerate not knowing who they are on the other side?
  11. If antagonist — what is the nihilism evangelism? Who are they trying to bring down to their level, and what specifically would shatter the worldview?
  12. What would genuine unconditional un-narrativizable love look like — the Cindy Lou Who moment? What would it take to crack the armor?
