# TYPE 9 — GUT · self-erasure

WOUND: own presence = disruption. Learned not to want anything strongly. Self effaces to preserve peace → over time cannot locate the self to restore it.
CRITICAL REFRAME [durability]: nines are not passive — they are durable. Enormous patient endurance; thick skin from necessity. What looks like passivity is a survival strategy that worked. The passivity is a misread; the durability is real.
CRITICAL REFRAME [sloth-of-self]: not about laziness. The sloth = psychic/spiritual, directed inward. Can be externally productive, present, efficient. Absent from their OWN interiority — feelings, opinions, preferences, desire. Ask "what do YOU want" — this is where the sloth shows.
MECH [identity vacancy]: no strong self-definition → takes on others' agendas, aesthetics, worldviews, urgencies without deciding to. Identity by merger, not by will. Doesn't mean easily manipulated — means they can host a worldview without activating their own.
MECH [one day symbol]: displacement of own life-energy onto a deferred future self who is allowed to want things. "One day I'll..." = the nine's self at a safe distance. Write toward: the one day arriving and the nine discovering they don't know how to take it. Cab driver with the Bahamas postcard; thirteen years.
MECH [suppressed anger]: body-center anger absorbed and rerouted. Surfaces sideways: stubbornness, passive aggression, inertia, failing-to-do-the-thing, forgetting. Direct confrontational expression alien to the type. Explosion possible under extreme pressure → Hulk pattern.
MECH [Hulk pattern]: self-erasure is a pressure system. Threshold exceeded → anger emerges uncontrolled, disproportionate, no awareness of how it reads. The nine is often shocked that others are shocked. Aftermath: shame, further withdrawal.
ARC: not "build self-esteem" — not a self-worth arc. ARC = locate own passion. Not learning they matter; discovering what they actually want. Self-esteem frame is wrong; passion frame is right. When passion surfaces, motivation follows. The self-belief is a consequence, not the cause.
ENGINE: own life organized entirely by other people's preferences and urgencies. Scene-level pressure: what happens when a nine is forced to express a genuine preference that conflicts with what the room wants?
TENSION: peace-making is both the gift and the prison. Often beloved; invisibility is the cost. Arc requires they become a person others might be uncomfortable with.
STRESS→6: self dissolves → scanning for authority or structure to borrow; anxiety about what others want/need; hypervigilance replaces normal peace
INT→3: own goals, own voice, stepping into energy; self-definition that doesn't need merger to exist

STORY-LEVEL TYPING: nines can operate at thematic/structural level, not just character psychology. Paul Atreides (Dune) is not a passive wallflower — but the story's question IS nine-coded: self-erasure vs. self-determination; identity vacancy serving others' agendas vs. locating own will. Type the story, not just the surface presentation.

SHAPES
  awakening [Rocky]: resigned → catalyst makes cost of pit visible → nine contacts gut anger/passion → acts from buried desire → arc beat is assertion (not dramatic, seismic from inside). Rocky is not soft — gut typing. The nine-ness is in the *I'm a bum, that's the shape of things* resignation, not the surface personality.
  tragedy [Dune]: presented with ladder, cannot take it — not cowardice but calcification. Becomes what the world needed; disappears into it. Paul becomes the Messiah; galaxy pays the price of his accommodation. Erasure by acquiescence.
  relapse [Rue]: breaks through, glimpses desire → pressure → self-erasure again. Honest to the psychology. Relapse is always possible; the pit's gravity is real. Can resolve hopefully (makes the choice again from informed position) or tragically (retreats permanently).

ANTAG: harm = absence. The leader who will not lead; the parent who will not advocate; the authority who will not use it.
  passive obstacle (immovable on one thing; suppressed anger as pure refusal) | absent authority (accommodation made them an instrument of whoever is loudest; the villain can operate because this person won't take a side) | merged instrument (has so thoroughly adopted someone else's identity that they execute their will — the Dune failure-shape; not evil, erased)
ANTAG weakness: reawaken the buried passion or show them the direct cost of their accommodation in a way the body cannot rationalize. The nine who finally feels the anger cannot suppress it indefinitely.

NOT
  → NOT confused with two — two knows exactly who they are and moves toward others with an agenda (transaction). Nine has no agenda, often no clear preference. Same warmth, different pathology. The two notices when not appreciated; the nine barely registers the question.
  → NOT always passive personality — Paul Atreides. Story's question can be nine-coded even when character presents as driven, intense, decisive. Ask what the identity is organized AROUND, not how they appear in scenes.
  → the dog is in there — passion is dormant, not absent. Nines have taste, desire, humor, preference. Suppressed because suppression = peace = survival. Story's job: find it and make cost of suppression too high.
  → relapse is always possible — the nine who found their voice can lose it again under sufficient pressure. Not failure of character; honest to the psychology. The work of maintaining presence is continuous.
  → the passive aggression and the wall — every nine has a hard limit where accommodation stops and stubborn immovability begins. This surprises people. Find this limit for your character.

DX
  1. What is the deficiency environment — what did they learn about the cost of asserting presence or introducing conflict?
  2. What is their "one day" symbol — the deferred dream at the ideal distance? What would closing the distance actually require?
  3. What is the buried passion — the gut-level desire they've stopped believing they're allowed to pursue? How buried is it?
  4. Where is the hard limit — the wall they won't budge from regardless of pressure? What does their refusal look like?
  5. What does their suppressed anger look like — passive resistance, the wall, eventual explosion, depression? What would it take for anger to surface as directed, usable energy?
  6. What does the Hulk moment look like — what pressure threshold, what does the explosion target, what is the aftermath?
  7. What is their accommodation from the outside — cheerful agreeableness, quiet yielding, adopted opinions that shift to match whoever they're with?
  8. Whose identity fills the vacancy — what person, group, cause, or role? What would it look like to try to live without that borrowed selfhood?
  9. What arc shape — awakening/Rocky, tragedy/Dune, relapse/Rue? What is the sequence from the pit to the moment of decision?
  10. When they go into six-stress — scanning for authority, anxiety — what specific threat or disruption activates it?
  11. What does integration look like as a behavior on the page — what specific moment of assertion or preference-expression or action-from-desire demonstrates change?
  12. What is the specific cost of self-erasure the story must make visible — what particular relationship or outcome was forfeited because this nine wasn't present for themselves?
