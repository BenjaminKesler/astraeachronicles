# Temptation — Act 3 Alternative to Disaster + Dark Moment
# source: "How To Write a TEMPTATION MOMENT (Alternative to Disaster)" — Abbie Emmons — https://youtu.be/ceZbrDrecFg
# authority: Weiland (Creating Character Arcs), McKee (Story), Truby (Anatomy of Story), Bell (Plot & Structure), Snyder (Save the Cat), Hauge (Hero's 2 Journeys)
# synthesis: [[wound-model-thesis]], [[wound-protective-mechanisms]], [[internal-conflict]], [[scene-earning]]
# cross-references: [[Beats/10_disaster_dark_moment]], [[Beats/11_aha_moment]], [[Beats/12_climactic_confrontation]]
# position: ~75–80% (same position as Disaster; replaces the Disaster + Dark Moment + Aha unit as the transformation trigger; Aha follows immediately)

---

PATH SELECTOR
  two paths to the Aha Moment — both wound-model-grounded, structurally distinct from each other
  STANDARD PATH ("stripped of the lie"):
    exogenous destruction — accumulated wound-driven decisions produce self-caused catastrophe; mechanism BROKEN
    "while still avoiding fear" disproved catastrophically → [[Beats/10_disaster_dark_moment]]
  TEMPTATION PATH ("found something truer"):
    endogenous dissolution — abstract desire grows real until mechanism becomes irrelevant; mechanism ERODED
    "while still avoiding fear" superseded — made irrelevant rather than disproved → this file
  SELECTION CRITERION: does the arc's transformation arrive through catastrophic stripping or through finding something genuinely better?
  → [[story-beats.md]] PATH SELECTOR for full application guidance

---

WOUND LAYER
state: mechanism progressively eroded (not broken catastrophically); abstract desire semi-legible; protagonist offered exact misbelief-based goal-object; double discovery (resentment gone + desire deflated); voluntary irreversibility; Temptation as precondition; Aha breaks the pull

  ENDOGENOUS DISSOLUTION (core mechanism — distinct from standard path)
    mechanism doesn't break in one catastrophic event; erodes progressively through Acts 2-3 as abstract desire grows more real
    wound-driven journey note: protagonist's surface desire pursuit accidentally produces transformation conditions —
      the act of chasing the goal-object puts protagonist in proximity with what they actually need
    result at Temptation: mechanism can no longer reach for the offered prize; desire has already changed through the journey

  RESENTMENT-FADING SIGNAL (upstream marker of dissolution progress)
    resentment about not having the misbelief-based goal = emotional signal the wound's amplification is still active
    present in early Act 2 at baseline; fades progressively across Act 2B as abstract desire grows and mechanism erodes
    character not consciously tracking this — the Temptation moment reveals it has already happened
    reader has felt the resentment too; its disappearance is part of the co-transformation

  DOUBLE DISCOVERY (the Temptation beat's specific content)
    offer arrives → protagonist expects familiar desire response → discovers it is different:
    FIRST: the resentment is gone — wound's amplification has dissolved without character realizing it
    SECOND: the desire is smaller than expected — wound's amplification inflated the surface desire;
      dissolution reveals the desire's actual un-inflated size
    both discoveries happen in the moment of being offered the thing; not consciously accumulated before this beat
    the gap between expected desire-size and actual desire-size IS the Aha's cognitive content

  "WHILE STILL AVOIDING FEAR" — SUPERSEDED (not disproved)
    standard path: assumption disproved catastrophically at Disaster — every wound-safe route finally exhausted
    temptation path: assumption becomes irrelevant as abstract desire grows more compelling through the journey
    fear loses grip gradually without being dramatically exposed; assumption fades rather than shatters
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] "while still avoiding fear" — Temptation path fork

  VOLUNTARY IRREVERSIBILITY (Bell's Doorway — inverted directionality)
    standard path: doors close BEHIND protagonist — structural, involuntary, protagonist unaware
    temptation path: final door is presented OPEN — protagonist can clearly see it and walk through (take the prize)
    protagonist chooses to close it — irreversibility authored from inside rather than imposed from outside
    maps directly to endogenous dissolution: the mechanism is released voluntarily, not stripped by external force
    permanent: once the goal-object is turned down, the misbelief-based goal is irrevocably closed
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] IRREVERSIBILITY — Doorway #1 (contrast: that door closed involuntarily)

  TWO-BEAT STRUCTURE: TEMPTATION + AHA (non-negotiable)
    Temptation = PRECONDITION: the offer, the residual pull, the dwell time, the double discovery
    Aha = RESOLUTION: cognitive crystallization breaks the pull; abstract desire fully legible through the act of refusal
    DWELL TIME REQUIREMENT: protagonist must sit in the diminished-but-present pull long enough for it to be a distinct beat
      structural equivalent of Dark Moment's forced confrontation — precondition cannot be rushed
      length proportional to upstream seeding: stronger seeding → more intense pull → longer dwell warranted
      the pull must be felt before the Aha can break it; if protagonist pre-empts it, the Aha has nothing to resolve
    → [[Beats/11_aha_moment]] Temptation path variant: preceding state; inverted three-shift sequence

HERO'S JOURNEY DISAMBIGUATION (non-negotiable framing)
  HJ temptation: test of moral STRENGTH — character still wants the thing; refuses through willpower; appears soon after FPP
  3-Act temptation: proof of TRANSFORMATION — desire itself has changed; refusal comes from growth, not will
  the protagonist doesn't resist because they should; they don't want it anymore because they've grown into something better
  diagnostic: is the protagonist resisting something they still want (HJ) or turning down something they've grown past (3-Act)?
  → failure mode: HERO'S JOURNEY CONFUSION

GENRE LAYER
state: no value floor in Temptation path; complex bittersweet charge at Temptation moment; bittersweet-to-positive emotional register

  VALUE CHAIN — TEMPTATION PATH (McKee)
    standard path: II → FPP → GCM → Disaster (FLOOR — most negative) → Aha (first positive in Act 3) → Climax (maximum)
    temptation path: II → FPP → GCM → [complex bittersweet charge at Temptation/Aha] → Climax (maximum)
    NO FLOOR — structural feature, not execution choice; value charge never hits its most negative point
    COMPLEX BITTERSWEET CHARGE at Temptation moment (all three simultaneously):
      positive: the goal-object is finally offered — the anticipated "this is it" moment
      bittersweet: desire has changed; protagonist cannot reach for it the way they expected
      positive: something genuinely better is already present
    resulting register: BITTERSWEET-TO-POSITIVE (not devastating-to-triumphant)
    explains genre tendency toward lighter/comedic: no floor-to-ceiling reversal; maximum emotional contrast is lower;
      transformation equally real, catharsis arrives in a quieter register
    Roman Holiday's bittersweet ending is this value structure made visible

  GENRE TENDENCY
    more common in lighthearted/comedic; can work in serious tones when the story shape matches
    "found something truer" → Temptation path; "stripped of the lie" → standard path

SYMPATHY LAYER
state: reader has been co-transforming alongside protagonist across Acts 2-3; both arrive at changed desire simultaneously; anticipatory "finally!" converts to shared complex recognition

  CO-TRANSFORMATION READER MECHANIC (distinct from standard Aha sympathy)
    standard Aha sympathy: reader held information advantage from Hook; watches protagonist finally catch up — VINDICATION
    Temptation Aha sympathy: reader has been experiencing abstract desire growing alongside protagonist; no information gap
    both reader and protagonist discover the changed desire TOGETHER in the moment of the offer
    reader also experiences the "finally!" anticipation when offer arrives — then discovers alongside protagonist that desire has changed
    emotional effect: SHARED DISCOVERY ("we both found this") rather than vindication ("I knew this all along")

  ANTICIPATORY PULL REQUIREMENT
    reader must feel the anticipatory pull when offer arrives — if reader has already evolved past the goal with no residual
      recognition, the "finally!" moment is absent and co-transformation mechanic cannot fire
    prevention: goal-object tracked visibly across Acts 2-3; reader's accumulated investment in the object is what makes
      the refusal land with full emotional weight → [[scene-earning]] planted resonance; distribution principle

---

RELATIONAL LAYER
function: RELATIONAL INTEGRITY — refusal protects or affirms what the journey built; moral reckoning and reconciliation collapse into one moment

  standard path: moral reckoning at Disaster (backward-facing) + reconciliation/opening at Aha (forward-facing) — two distinct beats
  temptation path: both collapse into the Temptation/Aha unit; the refusal IS the act of relational integrity
    simultaneously acknowledging cost (moral reckoning) and opening genuine engagement (reconciliation)
  the relationship that grew during abstract desire surfacing — the source of the protagonist's transformation —
    is typically what the refusal protects or honors
  design requirement: the relationship built through the journey should be visible at the Temptation moment;
    in some sense the protagonist is choosing it
  CALIBRATION: refusal is visible action; relational proof demonstrated, not stated

---

FAILURE MODES
  NO GENUINE PULL: protagonist refuses without feeling the offer's pull; not a temptation, just a rejection;
    co-transformation mechanic cannot fire; reader gets no "finally!" moment; impact deflated
    prevention: goal-object must be established and tracked so the offer lands with full anticipated weight

  PREMATURE REFUSAL (dwell time skipped): rushing from offer to refusal without sitting in the pull;
    Aha has nothing to break; equivalent to premature Aha in the standard path
    fix: protagonist must grapple with the diminished-but-present pull before cognitive crystallization arrives

  CHANGED PREFERENCE WITHOUT MORAL DIMENSION: "I don't want this anymore" without integrity;
    Truby's moral self-revelation absent; transformation reads as shallow preference-shift
    fix: design the refusal as "I choose not to take this because it would require me to be someone I'm no longer willing to be"

  HERO'S JOURNEY CONFUSION: protagonist resists through willpower and moral strength rather than changed desire;
    reads as a test of character (HJ temptation) not proof of transformation (3-Act Temptation)
    diagnostic: is there desire left to resist, or has the desire itself changed?

  GOAL-OBJECT MISMATCH: Temptation offers something adjacent to or a variant of the crystallized goal-object
    rather than the exact thing; reader doesn't recognize the bookend; impact deflated
    prevention: same specific object established at II; not upgraded, not symbolic — the exact thing

  UNDRAMATIZED WHIFF OF DEATH: internal desire fades quietly without visible external container;
    reader doesn't feel the loss; internal deaths are harder to dramatize than external ones
    fix: the external goal-object being refused must be visible enough that reader can feel what's dying

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  EXTERNAL GOAL-OBJECT (non-negotiable):
    surface desire must be externalized into a specific concrete visible thing — job, relationship, achievement, money, status
    established at the II as the desire that crystallized; SAME EXACT OBJECT offered at the Temptation
    tracked visibly across Acts 2-3 as planted resonance → [[scene-earning]] distribution principle
    when refused: four wound-model layers completed in one visible act:
      goal-object refused (external, visible) → surface desire permanently closed → misbelief translation released →
      abstract desire cleared to be pursued honestly (forward-facing)

  ABSTRACT DESIRE SEEDING (Temptation path-specific; critical difference from standard path):
    standard path: abstract desire fully subterranean until Aha — the Dark Moment's stripping does the work
    temptation path: abstract desire must be progressively surfacing through Acts 2-3; reader and protagonist co-transforming
    the co-transformation reader mechanic depends entirely on this seeding; without it neither has evolved past the goal
    seeding vehicle: dual-desire tension track across Act 2B — abstract desire growing alongside surface desire pursuit
    Temptation path does NOT protect abstract desire from legibility through Act 2B — active surfacing is required

  MORAL DIMENSION (Truby — both layers required):
    psychological: "I understand myself differently" — desire has changed; amplification is gone
    moral: "I will live differently" — I choose not to take this because integrity requires refusal
    design explicitly for both; the moral dimension makes this a choice, not only a discovery

  WHIFF OF DEATH — GOAL-OBJECT:
    the desire crystallized at the II permanently dies here — the specific goal the protagonist has been pursuing
    this is a VOLUNTARY INTERNAL death: protagonist releases it; contrasts with standard path's external/imposed loss
    design challenge: internal deaths harder to dramatize than external; external goal-object container is the solution
    diagnostic: what specifically dies — and is it visible through the external container being refused?

  CLIMAX DESIGN NOTE:
    the turning-down CAN function as the Climax if staged under maximum external pressure
    minimum requirements: story's highest-stakes external confrontation; protagonist acts against old wound-driven
      identity consequentially; visible to characters who matter; refusal at these stakes = proof function fires
    if refusal happens in low-stakes circumstances: Aha function only; Climax still owed as a separate beat
    Roman Holiday model: private decision (Aha) → public press conference refusal (Climax) — two distinct beats
    → [[Beats/12_climactic_confrontation]] Temptation path Climax option

  POSITION INHERITANCE
    from Second PP: mechanism progressively irrelevant (not strained under pressure); abstract desire nearly equal to
      surface desire; resentment about goal-object now significantly faded; board set for Temptation offer, not for Disaster
    from Post-Midpoint Action Hero: dual-desire tension running; resentment fading from Act 2B baseline;
      wound-driven pursuit accidentally producing transformation conditions
    from GCM: abstract desire first consciously felt; protagonist can no longer pretend they don't want something different;
      Doorway #2 in Temptation direction — irreversibility runs toward the abstract desire, not away from the wound-safe route
    from FPP: goal-object crystallized at II (what Temptation will offer back); "while still avoiding fear" committed;
      abstract desire chain forked here toward progressive surfacing
    from Hook: abstract desire incongruity leak (Hauge) planted more deliberately than in standard path — seed for
      progressive surfacing; mechanism at full strength (baseline from which erosion arc descends)
    what this beat opens:
      → [[Beats/11_aha_moment]] Temptation variant: cognitive crystallization through act of refusal; abstract desire fully legible
      → [[Beats/12_climactic_confrontation]] first action from dissolved-misbelief state; potential Climax collapse if staged at max pressure

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  what is the specific external goal-object? is it established at the II and tracked visibly across Acts 2-3? (goal-object check)
  is the offer the exact same object — not an adjacent variant or upgrade? (exactness check)
  does the protagonist feel genuine pull when the offer arrives, or do they refuse immediately? (dwell time check)
  is the resentment about the goal-object visible in Act 2 and fading across Act 2B? (resentment mechanism check)
  what is the moral dimension of the refusal — act of integrity, not just changed preference? (Truby moral check)
  are both self-revelation layers present — psychological AND moral? (Truby two-layer check)
  is the abstract desire semi-legible before the Temptation — has the dual-desire tension been running through Act 2B? (seeding check)
  does the turning-down permanently close the door — is voluntary irreversibility felt by the reader? (Bell's doorway check)
  what specifically dies — and is it visible through the external goal-object container? (whiff of death check)
  is the Aha distinct from the Temptation — does the pull get broken rather than pre-empted? (two-beat structure check)
  is the value charge complex bittersweet rather than purely positive or purely negative? (McKee check)
  is the refusal an act of relational integrity — does it protect or affirm what the journey built? (relational check)
  if intended as Climax: is this the story's maximum external pressure moment? (Climax collapse check)
