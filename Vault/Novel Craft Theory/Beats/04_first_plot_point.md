# First Plot Point — Act 1 Beat 4
# source: "How to Write the First Plot Point of a Story" — Abbie Emmons — https://youtu.be/5olLwyjec5I
# authority: Weiland (Structuring Your Novel / Creating Character Arcs), Field (Screenplay), Snyder (Save the Cat), McKee (Story), Hauge (Hero's 2 Journeys), Vogler (Writer's Journey), Bell (Plot & Structure), Truby (Anatomy of Story), Cron (Story Genius)
# synthesis: [[wound-model-thesis]], [[internal-conflict]], [[wound-protective-mechanisms]], [[universal-diagnostics]], [[plot-structure-outlining]]
# cross-references: [[Beats/01_hook]], [[Beats/02_inciting_incident]], [[Beats/03_build_up]]
# position: ~25% (end of Act 1; Build-up resolves into this; first structural hinge of the full arc)

---

WOUND LAYER
state: tipping point reached; inaction pain > perceived action pain; misbelief at the wheel; protagonist commits to venturing out while still believing wound-avoidance is achievable; first link in causal chain to disaster; mechanism intact but navigating active commitment for first time (not only defense)

  PAIN VS. PAIN (the decision mechanism)
    brain always chooses least painful of available options — never truly "does the harder thing"; only rewrites which thing is harder
    tipping point: subconscious comparison runs constantly until threshold: inaction pain > action pain → protagonist commits
    the feeling of the action doesn't change; only what it's being compared to does — neither bravery nor growth; a recalculation
    inaction side of scale: built by Hook's comfort zone + Build-up's accumulating inaction cost
    action side of scale: perceived as manageable because misbelief has minimized it ("I can still route around my wound")
    → [[Beats/03_build_up]] pain-floor exception (same mechanism in terse form); [[Beats/01_hook]] comfort zone forward dependency

  "WHILE STILL AVOIDING FEAR" (the structural load of Act 2)
    protagonist ventures out believing they can get the desire AND sidestep the wound — fatal assumption
    misbelief's calculation: the wound-safe route exists; I don't have to confront what I'm most afraid of
    this assumption is precisely what Act 2 systematically disproves — each beat removes one wound-safe option
    "the one move that would actually work is always the move they won't make" → [[wound-model-thesis]] Part 3
    the FPP commitment doesn't just open Act 2 — it specifies what Act 2 must disprove; the route chosen here determines the obstacle chain
    → [[Beats/03_build_up]] "while still avoiding fear" note (seeded there; structural engine named here)

  VALUES REVELATION (opaque abstract desire exposure)
    FPP = first moment abstract desire becomes faintly legible — not stated, not conscious, not named
    the pain calculation is filtered through a values screen, not only a fear screen (Truby)
    the commitment resonates with what the character actually needs, even as they pursue it through the wrong solution
    mechanism: kernel of genuine value buried inside the misbelief-steered choice — "right reason, wrong method"
    distinct from aha moment: FPP = opaque single note; aha = full dissolution and clear sight
    chain: Hook (incongruity leak, Hauge) → II/Build-up (subterranean) → FPP (opaque) → aha (fully legible)
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] Hauge nuance (abstract desire leak as incongruity); [[wound-model-thesis]] Part 4

  IRREVERSIBILITY — DOORWAY OF NO RETURN #1 (Bell)
    FPP closes a door the protagonist doesn't know has closed
    protagonist believes Option A (retreat) is still available if the plan fails — the Build-up's refusal stage made it feel viable
    structurally: the ordinary world is now inaccessible; the story has permanently foreclosed retreat
    tension: protagonist's subjective sense of open door vs. story's objective closed door
    this gap is the engine of Act 2 desperation: protagonist keeps trying to find an exit that no longer exists
    Weiland / Bell / Vogler converge independently: FPP is a doorway of no return, regardless of character's perception
    → [[Beats/03_build_up]] retreat option seeding mistaken belief in reversibility
    → [[Beats/07_game_changing_midpoint]] Doorway #2 — second irreversibility closes the wound-safe route at the midpoint

---

GENRE LAYER (last constitutive genre beat)
state: genre is still actively defining what the commitment means; after FPP, genre becomes validating (consistency check), not load-bearing
  protagonist's commitment must be genre-shaped: what they risk and what they pursue must belong to this genre's value system
  constitutive function: reader must know what protagonist is walking into — specific, world-grounded, not abstract
  FPP = last beat where genre does structural work; Act 2 operates inside an already-established world
  → [[Beats/01_hook]] genre layer (genre promise + constitutive role established there)
  → [[Beats/03_build_up]] consequence architecture (what this world does to people who make this choice — now activating)

VALUE CHARGE SHIFT (McKee — distinct from genre promise)
  every structural turning point produces a reader-perceivable shift in the global value charge
  without this shift, FPP can be mechanically correct but not felt as a structural beat by the reader
  II produced the first value swing (global value put in play); FPP produces the second, irreversible swing; GCM produces the third → [[Beats/07_game_changing_midpoint]] value charge shift
  shift doesn't have to be simple positive↔negative: charge within same pole counts (stable-negative → committed-danger)
  diagnostic: what was the charge before? what is it after? can the reader feel the difference without being told?
  → [[Beats/02_inciting_incident]] genre layer (first value swing); [[Beats/01_hook]] genre layer (global value established)

---

SYMPATHY LAYER
state: dramatic irony at maximum; reader holds more than character at highest intensity in Act 1

  reader holds: wound model, tipping point logic, "while still avoiding fear" assumption, AND structural irreversibility
  character holds: none of the above — makes a decision that looks rational from inside the misbelief
  gap = deepest "aching for" in Act 1; reader watching someone commit intelligently to a pre-loaded failure
  CHARACTER LENS REQUIREMENT (non-negotiable)
    decision must look rational from inside the misbelief
    if decision looks dumb to reader → characterization failure, not dramatic irony; identification collapses
    "while still avoiding fear" is what makes the commitment look rational: protagonist genuinely believes the path delivers both goal and wound-avoidance
    → [[universal-diagnostics]] character lens audit; [[plot-structure-outlining]] misbelief-steered plan (same requirement at Act 2B)
  → [[Beats/03_build_up]] sympathy layer (dramatic irony activated there, maximized here)

---

RELATIONAL LAYER
function: COMMITMENT COST — relational consequence of FPP decision is now locked in even if still unseen

  character commits; someone in their world absorbs the first cost of that commitment
  character cannot yet register this
  some relationships get recruited into the wound-safe plan; others get left behind or put at risk
  distinction from Build-up's FIRST MORAL WEIGHT:
    Build-up: relational cost implied by the choice being faced — possible, not yet real
    FPP: decision made — cost is now accumulating; the relational consequence is locked in even if unnamed
  → [[Beats/03_build_up]] relational layer (first moral weight → commitment cost)

  CALIBRATION
    subtext principle: consequence implied by the commitment — reader infers; character doesn't yet feel it
    embed rule: relational dimension lives within the commitment scene, not a standalone relationship scene
    weight: slightly more present than Build-up; pattern beginning to compound
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] relational layer for universal calibration principles

---

STAKES LEGIBILITY (audience-facing requirement — distinct from wound model architecture)
  after FPP, reader must be able to answer three questions:
    (1) what does the protagonist want? (desire crystallization from II — should already be answered)
    (2) what is preventing them? (obstacle chain now set in motion by the commitment)
    (3) what happens if they fail? (price of failure — the gap most easily missed)
  (3) is not the same as wound architecture: stakes must be communicated outward, not only felt inward by the character
  diagnostic: can a reader who doesn't know the wound model answer all three after this scene?
  → [[Beats/02_inciting_incident]] story question activation (Weiland — FPP is where that question gets its first answer)

---

FAILURE MODES
PUNCHING BAG (plot makes the decision)
  plot forces protagonist out of comfort zone; no genuine agency at the commitment moment
  world may constrain options (Sense and Sensibility — they cannot stay in the house); character's choice WITHIN those constraints defines the beat
  no agency → character still being acted upon at the story's first hinge; Act 2 has no wound-driven engine
  → [[Beats/03_build_up]] failure mode: PUNCHING BAG (parallel; same root cause, different beat)

PAIN IMBALANCE (tipping point has no weight)
  one side of the scale is empty → no real tipping point → commitment feels arbitrary or convenient
  inaction side empty: comfort zone wasn't made real → Hook failure; fix upstream
  action side empty: obstacle/risk not established → II or Build-up failure; fix upstream
  symptom: protagonist appears to go along with the plot rather than make a painful calculation

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  character lens audit at this beat: is the misbelief actually shaping this specific commitment?
  tipping point earned: both sides of the scale need prior investment from Hook and Build-up
  "while still avoiding fear" must be visible in some form — reader needs to register the assumption even if character can't
  decision must be genre-shaped AND produce a reader-perceivable value shift — both required; neither alone sufficient
  dramatized vs. internalized: commitment need not be externally visible; minimum = reader registers it as real
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] character lens audit; [[Beats/03_build_up]] dramatized vs. internalized

  POSITION INHERITANCE
    from Build-up: both fear layers active, comfort zone collapsed, impossible choice in view, refusal stage complete, tipping point imminent
    from II: crystallized surface desire as the specific goal being committed to; story question raised
    from Hook: comfort zone made real (feeds tipping point inaction side); mechanism at full strength (now navigating active commitment); abstract desire incongruity leak (seed of values revelation)
    what FPP opens: Act 2 compression chamber; the specific wound-safe route chosen here determines what Act 2 must systematically close off
      → [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]] (wound-safe route is the antagonist's calibration target at the First PP)
      → [[Beats/06_pre_midpoint_reactionary_hero]] (wound-safe route becomes the stupid plan; executes through this phase; terminates at midpoint)
      → [[Beats/07_game_changing_midpoint]] (Doorway #2 — wound-safe route terminated here; protagonist state first shifts in Act 2)

---

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  what specific misbelief calculation makes this commitment "less painful" than staying? (tipping point check)
  what exactly does the protagonist believe they can still avoid while pursuing the goal? (while still avoiding fear check)
  what wound-safe route has the misbelief selected — what does Act 2 need to disprove? (Act 2 engine check)
  does the decision look rational from inside the misbelief? (character lens audit)
  what door has just closed that the protagonist doesn't know about? (irreversibility check)
  what global value has shifted — and can the reader feel it without being told? (value charge check)
  can a reader answer: what do they want, what's stopping them, what's the price of failure? (stakes legibility check)
  does reader hold more than character — and is character's logic airtight from their own POV? (dramatic irony check)
  what relational consequence is now locked in, even if no character has named it? (commitment cost check)
