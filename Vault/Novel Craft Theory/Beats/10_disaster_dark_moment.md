THREE-BEAT UNIT
  Disaster / Dark Moment / Aha form a miniature three-act structure nested within Act 3:
    Disaster = inciting incident of the internal transformation
    Dark Moment = compressed Act 2 of the inner arc; protagonist at absolute rock bottom
    Aha = resolution of the inner arc; abstract desire finally legible
  cannot evaluate or design any one in isolation; all three must be co-designed
  → [[Beats/11_aha_moment]] (pending next video)

---

SUPPOSED VICTORY (optional pre-Disaster)
  protagonist believes the plan has worked; misbelief at maximum commitment — they believe they've proven the misbelief true
  makes Disaster more devastating; also sharpens the wound-model mechanism: protagonist most certain = most exposed
  distinct from GCM false victory (midpoint position, different function) — do not conflate
  EXECUTION RISK: scale of false confidence must match depth of wound-model grounding; overplaying without precise
    calibration tips into melodrama; larger play = higher precision demand
  diagnostic: why does my protagonist believe right now that they've already won?

---

WOUND LAYER
DISASTER state: "while still avoiding fear" assumption finally and completely disproved; dominant mechanism breaks (not strained — breaks); self-causer chain completes; protagonist makes final wound-driven decision that completes their own ruin; raw wound directly exposed with all routing removed
DARK MOMENT state: misbelief-as-refuge gone; dominant mechanism stripped; protagonist in most unfamiliar psychological state of entire story; no practiced defense; forced confrontation with kryptonite; abstract desire obstruction removed but not yet legible

  SELF-CAUSER COMPLETION (core mechanism)
    protagonist's final wound-driven decision is the last link in the causal chain from FPP forward
    every wound-driven decision since FPP has accumulated causally to this moment
    reader has had front-row seat to the entire chain; reader satisfaction = causation validated, not suffering inflicted
    protagonist is not a punching bag — they are more or less punching themselves

  "WHILE STILL AVOIDING FEAR" — FINAL DISPROOF
    the structural assumption committed to at the FPP and operative through all of Act 2 fully and irrevocably false
    the wound-safe route was never real; the compression chamber has exhausted every option
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] "while still avoiding fear" (named there as Act 2 engine; fully disproved here)

  KRYPTONITE (target of the Disaster)
    not the surface fear — the raw wound when the dominant mechanism is stripped
    "forced into the ring": inescapable confrontation; protagonist cannot route, avoid, overcompensate, or surrender
    TWO-FEAR-LAYER CONVERGENCE: both layers hit simultaneously at the Disaster
      external fear: misbelief's predicted consequences finally arrive as reality
      internal fear: raw wound directly activated (kryptonite)
    Enneagram kryptonite per type = springboard for Disaster design; intensity must be scaled up
    → [[internal-conflict]] TWO FEAR LAYERS; [[wound-protective-mechanisms]] arc diagnostic

  CRISIS AS DECISION (McKee)
    Disaster is not purely passive: protagonist makes a final wound-driven decision under maximum pressure
    the hardest decision of the story — made from inside the misbelief at its most threatened
    this decision is what puts them in the Dark Moment specifically: sitting in the wreckage of their own last choice
    truth-driven decision (the mirror) belongs to the Aha → [[Beats/11_aha_moment]] (pending)

  DARK MOMENT AS FORCED CONFRONTATION
    not merely broken or hopeless — protagonist cannot route, look away, or defend
    the thing they've spent the entire story running from is now the only thing in the room
    qualitatively different from sadness: no mechanism = no exits; required condition for revelation

---

GENRE LAYER
state: genre validating (consistency check), not constitutive; Disaster is genre's most negative expression
  what "everything goes wrong" means is genre-specific; Disaster must be genre-calibrated
  global value chain: II (first swing) → FPP (second, irreversible) → GCM (third) → Disaster (floor)
  → [[Beats/01_hook]] genre layer (global value established); [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] genre layer (last constitutive beat)

VALUE CHARGE SHIFT — GLOBAL FLOOR (McKee)
  Disaster = most negative global value charge in the entire story; more negative than anything in Acts 1 or 2
  diagnostic: if the Disaster doesn't produce the most negative value charge in the story, it isn't doing its structural job
  Dark Moment holds at the floor; Aha produces the first positive swing in Act 3

---

SYMPATHY LAYER
DISASTER state: reader's accumulated dramatic irony validated; foreseeability completed; satisfaction through confirmed understanding
DARK MOMENT state: protagonist most vulnerable and most human; identification at maximum because all defenses are gone

  reader has held the full causal chain from FPP forward; Disaster validates their understanding — they were right
  reader satisfaction ≠ enjoyment of suffering; it's the foreseeability cycle completing:
    reader has been forecasting this outcome; Disaster confirms the forecast; compulsion to find out if right → confirmed
  Dark Moment: reader watches protagonist face the kryptonite with no mechanism left
    most identification-rich state in the arc: defenses gone = character visible at their most essential
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] foreseeability cycle; mirror neurons vs. identification

---

RELATIONAL LAYER
function: MORAL RECKONING — relational cost loading since FPP comes due; protagonist confronted with what the wound-driven journey cost others

  not only "the plan failed" but "look what the plan did to the people around you" (Truby)
  protagonist confronts the moral cost of wound-driven behavior — distinct from wound exposure
  WHIFF OF DEATH (Snyder): something irrevocably dies at this beat — a relationship, an identity, a dream
    distinct from mechanism stripping; names the specific loss the reader and protagonist must grieve
    diagnostic: what is the specific thing that is gone after this beat and cannot be recovered?
  → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] relational layer (commitment cost — origin); [[Beats/09_second_pinch_point]] relational layer (bond under pressure → moral reckoning here)

  CALIBRATION
    subtext principle does not apply here — relational cost becomes explicit at the Disaster, not implied
    the pattern felt-but-unnamed through Acts 1 and 2 must now be faced directly
    → contrast with [[Beats/01_hook]] relational layer (lightest weight); Disaster is where the relational arc completes

---

FAILURE MODES
  SWAPPABLE DISASTER: disaster equally terrible for any character; doesn't connect to misbelief; mirror neurons not identification
    most common failure — fix by designing from the kryptonite inward
  PUNCHING BAG: disaster lands without protagonist's final wound-driven decision; self-causer chain incomplete; reader pity not identification
  GENERIC DARK MOMENT: protagonist simply sad rather than in forced confrontation; mechanism not fully stripped; some routing remains; revelation cannot land
  AHA MOMENT THEFT: disaster strips mechanism AND surfaces abstract desire explicitly; aha depleted before it arrives
    disaster removes the obstruction — it does not deliver the vision
  MELODRAMA (Supposed Victory variant): false confidence at larger scale than wound-model precision can support; tips into cliché

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  CO-DESIGN WITH SECOND PINCH POINT (non-negotiable)
    Second PP sets the board for protagonist's final all-in commitment; Disaster custom-crafted to meet and detonate it
    protagonist's only available misbelief-driven move at Second PP is to go all in
    Disaster draws out that desperation in a way that can only fail for this character in this way
    NOT the last cycle running out of steam — ends with punctuation: protagonist maximally committed → specific detonation
    design both beats in concert before finalizing either
    → [[Beats/09_second_pinch_point]] co-design requirement

  WHIFF OF DEATH (distinct from mechanism stripping)
    name the specific thing that dies at this beat for this character
    person, relationship, identity, dream — something irrevocable that reader and protagonist invested in
    diagnostic: what is specifically gone after this beat that cannot be recovered?

  KRYPTONITE CALIBRATION
    design from the raw wound inward: what emotionally disarms THIS protagonist when the mechanism is stripped?
    diagnostic: "how is my protagonist forced into the ring with their kryptonite?"

  COMFORT ZONE → DISASTER STRUCTURAL LOOP
    Hook made the comfort zone real and worth having — that groundwork pays interest here
    Disaster violates the comfort zone at its deepest level; first and last beats of the arc structurally echoed
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] comfort zone forward dependency (structural echo destination)

  THREE-BEAT UNIT DESIGN
    design Disaster, Dark Moment, and Aha before finalizing any one; they are one movement
    → [[Beats/11_aha_moment]] (pending next video)

  POSITION INHERITANCE
    from Second PP: felt enclosure + harder doubling down + self-causer pattern readable by reader + mechanism visibly
      strained + antagonist at near-maximum proximity + board set for final all-in commitment; relational bonds under pressure
    from Post-Midpoint Action Hero: every Act 2B competent-false-framed choice is part of the causal chain revealed here;
      abstract desire subterranean entering Disaster; seeding obligation terminates here
    from GCM: Doorway #2 committed; protagonist on wound-safe angle through Act 2B; relational cost loading since first visible cost
    from FPP: "while still avoiding fear" fully disproved here; values revelation chain: FPP (opaque) → Disaster (obstruction
      stripped) → Aha (fully legible); commitment cost loading since FPP becomes explicit moral reckoning
    from Hook: comfort zone made real and worth having — structurally echoed here; abstract desire incongruity leak (Hauge)
      chain completes at Aha
    what this beat opens: Aha Moment — abstract desire finally legible; misbelief dissolved; truth-driven decision possible
      for the first time → [[Beats/11_aha_moment]] (pending)

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  what does this disaster specifically mean to THIS protagonist — equally devastating to any character? (swappability test)
  what is the protagonist's final wound-driven decision that completes the self-causer chain? (crisis as decision check)
  what is this character's kryptonite — how does the disaster force them into the ring with it? (kryptonite check)
  what specifically dies at this beat — the irrevocable loss? (whiff of death check)
  have Second PP and Disaster been co-designed — does Second PP set the board for this specific detonation? (co-design check)
  does the disaster hit both fear layers — external (predicted consequences arrive) and internal (raw wound)? (two-fear-layer check)
  does the dark moment feel like forced confrontation, not just despair? (dark moment quality check)
  does the disaster strip the mechanism without surfacing the abstract desire? (aha moment theft check)
  is the global value charge at its most negative point in the entire story? (value floor check)
  if using Supposed Victory — is scale of false confidence matched by depth of wound-model grounding? (calibration check)
  what was the protagonist's comfort zone at the Hook — does the disaster violate it at its deepest level? (loop check)
  what is the moral reckoning — what did the wound-driven journey cost the people around the protagonist? (moral reckoning check)
