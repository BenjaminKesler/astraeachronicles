# TYPE 8 — GUT · armor-and-sovereignty

WOUND: vulnerability = door through which betrayal enters → seal the door. The betrayal wound is central and specific — not a vague fear of weakness, but a discrete early moment when being soft produced harm. The armor is specifically designed to prevent that from happening again.
MECH [armor gap]: armor is real and functional AND it was built over something. The story lives in the space between the armor's surface and what it's protecting. Engine activates when someone finds the seam.
MECH [ID-operating]: eights primarily operate from the ID — gut, body, instinct. Worst at the self-justification game. When philosophically backed into a corner, they don't produce articulate defenses — they act. The immature eight caught out is recognizable by the impulsive, inarticulate quality of someone whose ego is out of practice.
MECH [protector shadow]: the eight who must be the strongest person in the relationship often needs the people around them to remain relatively weak. Protection that requires dependence ≠ pure benevolence — it is sovereignty expressed as care. Story pressure: when the protected become powerful enough to not need protecting.
ARC: come down from the armor and let yourself be seen. NOT becoming gentle/passive — the fully integrated eight doesn't lose the fierceness. Discovers power can coexist with softness.
ENGINE: gap between armor and what the armor is protecting.
TENSION: surrendering control = existentially threatening. To be vulnerable = to be betrayable again. Eight's progress requires someone with enough force of their own that the eight doesn't feel they're giving up power — only choosing to share it.
MORALLY NEUTRAL ARC: the eight arc doesn't have to be moral improvement — it can be actualization. The character does a thing that, in the context of who they were and what they've overcome, represents genuine growth, even if the thing itself is arguably wrong. Reader feels the pull of both: warmth of the eight finally opening AND the horror of what that opening costs.
KEY ARC BEAT [emotional release]: cannot be willed. The eight who decides to be vulnerable is still operating from control. The release that matters happens *despite* the armor — when pressure produces feeling that cannot be contained. The dam breaking. Story must build pressure high enough that breaking is the only option.
STRESS→5: seriously threatened → withdrawal, secretive, isolates, hoards information, cuts people out, unreachable. The person who used to fill the room is now gone quiet. NOT integration — avoidance wearing a different coat. An eight gone five-ish under stress is planning.
INT→2: stops taking, starts giving. Force/intensity directed outward in genuine service. Capable of putting themselves second; submitting to a cause without needing to be its commander.

SHAPES
  vulnerability arc: common and most character-revealing. Context: almost always parenthood or love (domains where control fails most thoroughly). Key beat = the emotional release — the dam breaking moment.
  outlaw/leadership arc: detached from society; called back by necessity. Tension ≠ whether they'll act (they will); tension = whether they'll rejoin the community rather than just defend it from outside. Arc completes when they stay.
  fall from power arc: the tighter the grip, the faster the slipping. Alonzo Harris. The story must commit to the eight being pathetic at the end — the badass mythology does not protect from consequence.

ANTAG: damage is instinctual not strategic — dominate, not scheme. Subjugation = reflex, not plan.
  paranoid ruler (grip tightening → slipping → pathetic; Alonzo/Macbeth) | domineering protector (care becomes captivity when protected grow; mob-boss father shape) | force of nature (no visible inner life — just will/presence/force; the antagonism is existential: doesn't leave room for others' autonomy)
ANTAG weakness: find what they love. Armor = impenetrable to argument/force/strategy. The tender interior — the specific person or thing the armor is actually protecting — is the lever. Make them choose between it and their power.

NOT
  → eights CAN be pathetic — commit to this. The badass mythology is not permanent and not protection. An eight who cannot lose is not written.
  → power ≠ type — threes pursue power for status; ones because they're fit to hold it; sixes stumble into it. Eight is distinguished by the *relationship to control* (gut-level sovereignty need, refusal to be subjugated) NOT by power itself. The eight doesn't tell themselves a story about why they deserve power — they just take it.
  → non-eight environments are most revealing — placing them where their core tool (direct confrontation, force, will) is specifically unhelpful exposes their full humanity. Art world, diplomacy, relationship where power ≠ currency.
  → the protector shadow must be examined — "is this pure benevolence?" must always be asked. What happens when the protected become powerful enough to not need protecting almost always reveals the shadow.

DX
  1. What is the specific wound underneath the armor — the betrayal/punishment for softness that forged "never again"?
  2. What does their armor look like from the outside — physical intimidation, social dominance, control of information, aggressive directness?
  3. What is their relationship to the people they protect — enabling (trying to make them stronger) or requiring dependence? What happens when the protected grow?
  4. What closes the armor — what specific person/situation/quality creates the conditions for vulnerability? Who currently has even partial access?
  5. What does the ID-operating quality look like in practice — how does the impulsive, unmediated gut response manifest, and when does it get them in trouble?
  6. What arc shape fits this character — vulnerability/armor-down, outlaw-called-back, fall from power?
  7. What is the emotional release moment — the dam-breaking beat that cannot be controlled? What specific pressure is high enough? Who witnesses it?
  8. What is the thing they love that creates vulnerability — the person/cause/relationship the armor wasn't fully designed to protect? Who knows it? This is the lever.
  9. When this eight goes into five-stress — withdrawing, secretive, isolating — what are they planning? How does it read to people used to their forward presence?
  10. What does integration look like as a behavior — what would they submit to, and to whom? Where does the force turn into service?
  11. What is the specific non-eight environment that would most expose them — what situation requires tools they don't have?
  12. What is the specific cost of their armor the story must make visible — what particular person/outcome is the direct consequence of this character's refusal to be vulnerable?
