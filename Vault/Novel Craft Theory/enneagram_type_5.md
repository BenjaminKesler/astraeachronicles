# TYPE 5 — HEAD · resource-hoarding

WOUND: resource fear — world demands more than they have. Response: become sufficient. Acquire capacity (knowledge/skill/resources), not love or status.
MECH [avarice]: resource hoarding = time, energy, attention, space, emotional availability — NOT primarily financial. Rationing is the operating mode. Terror of depletion: "what happens if I run out?"
MECH [competence ego]: fives get off on preparedness. Genuine narcissistic satisfaction in knowing the thing, in being ready. When in their element they can become extremely brash and overconfident. NOT purely humble — the pride is part of what makes them resistant to growth.
MECH [emotional delay]: emotional experience is present but processed internally and asynchronously — arrives on delay. May love someone deeply and the person has no idea. A five who feels nothing is a caricature; a five who feels everything and has no idea how to express it in real time is a person.
ARC: spend before ready. The leap of faith = structural, not spiritual: act without the certainty they've been waiting for. Stop preparing and engage.
ENGINE: world keeps demanding more than the five believes they have. The model breaks → entire security threatened (theory IS security). Story job: find what requires the five to spend what they've been hoarding.
TENSION: competence vs. presence. The more they prepare to be capable, the less they actually engage. Readiness is an asymptote. Story must force action before preparation is complete.
STRESS→7: model breaks → scatter, frantic, unfocused, ungoverned. The controlled observer coming apart is more terrifying than the one in control.
INT→8: decisiveness, physical groundedness, confident action, willingness to step in and lead

SHAPES
  redeem: mystery arc (something incoherent that falsifies the theory → accept limits of model → doesn't destroy them) | love story arc (mystery is a person; Good Will Hunting) | burn-it-down arc (had to know so badly they destroy themselves; answer they couldn't find was the thing they were running from: connection)
  tragedy: preparation that never gets used — lifetime of accumulating competence, never deploying. Died alone in their head, knowing everything, having spent nothing.
  antag: operates from superior knowledge, menace = may actually be right. world-builder (model is reality) | hoarder-as-predator (weaponizes scarcity; everyone needs what they have) | observer who stopped caring (processes suffering as data; horror = absence not cruelty)

ANTAG weakness: specific person in front of them who cannot be filed into the model without breaking it. Argument = has counter-arguments. Falsification of the theory = no recovery.

NOT
  → NOT cold — delayed. Feels everything; processes asynchronously. "Cold" is a surface misread.
  → fives in world > fives in their heads. Theory alone ≠ story. Narrative only generates through collision with what they've been preparing to face.
  → NOT purely intellectual — martial artist, prepper, financial hoarder are all running the same psychology. Domain of competence varies; the *relationship* to competence (private, for internal security, not for display) is what matters.
  → the body often shows the neglect — unkempt, physically distracted, sometimes a distinctly singular esoteric style; grounds the abstraction on the page

DX
  1. What domain is competence organized around — and why that domain? What does mastery here specifically protect them from?
  2. What does their model of the world explain well — and what is it conspicuously unable to account for?
  3. What does the world keep producing that the model can't file — how have they been rationalizing it so far?
  4. What have they been preparing for that they haven't done yet — what keeps moving the readiness threshold?
  5. What is their relationship to their own emotional life — do they know what they're feeling in the moment, or does it arrive later?
  6. Who is the one trusted person they've allowed in, if anyone — how did that person earn access?
  7. What would the leap of faith look like — the specific moment they act before ready, commit before certain?
  8. What does their hoarding look like — time, knowledge, money, space, energy — which do they ration most tightly, and from whom?
  9. What happens when they stress toward seven — when the model breaks, what does the scatter look like?
  10. Where is the ego around competence — the pride in preparation — and how does it resist help or evidence that the model is incomplete?
  11. What does the physical self look like — where does the neglect of the somatic show on the page?
  12. What would actually spending what they've been saving look like — give the knowledge, offer the presence, make the commitment — and find themselves still standing?
