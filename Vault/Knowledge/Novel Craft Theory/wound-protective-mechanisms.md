# Wound-Protective Mechanisms
# source: synthesized from schema therapy (Young), Enneagram theory, Abbiee character series
# cross-reference: [[internal-conflict]] for wound formation model; [[wound-model-thesis]] for full synthesis

---

THREE MECHANISMS (Young's schema persistence modes)
  all serve same function: protecting the misbelief from challenge; delaying wound confrontation
  each character has a DOMINANT mechanism — their wound-protective signature, part of character design
  at scene level, active mechanism is CONTEXT-SENSITIVE — same wound, different activation by context

AVOIDANCE
  routes around any situation that would challenge the misbelief or expose the wound
  surface on page: retreat, refusal, detours — character steering clear
  reader experience: tension — the thing they're running from keeps getting closer

SURRENDER
  actively re-chooses the misbelief as psychological refuge under threat
  running toward false safety, not just failing to escape — the misbelief as comfort
  surface on page: self-sabotage, wrong-refuge-seeking, returning to the same well
  reader experience: empathetic frustration — you can see it isn't working

OVERCOMPENSATION
  performs the opposite of the wound's content to prove it doesn't exist
  fear of weakness → hypercompetent, domineering; fear of abandonment → fiercely self-sufficient
  most deceptive mechanism — can fool reader into thinking the character has their wound handled
  surface on page: apparent strength, mastery, invulnerability; armor that visibly strains
  reader experience: creeping dread
  disaster for overcompensation must crack the armor specifically — not just threaten the surface desire

---

SCENE-LEVEL ACTIVATION (context-sensitive around the dominant mechanism)
  relationship: same character may overcompensate with strangers, surrender with intimates
  threat level:
    low → wound not activated
    medium → dominant mechanism fires
    extreme → overwhelms dominant mechanism; backup fires raw and unpracticed
  arc position:
    early → mechanism intact, well-practiced
    mid → increasingly costly to maintain; character doubling down
    late → visibly strained; cracks visible to reader before character feels them

---

ARC DIAGNOSTIC
  mechanism shifts = visible signal of arc progression
    overcompensator who starts avoiding in Act 2 = armor cracking
    character still running identical mechanism across Acts 1 and 2 = arc not moving
  disaster = dominant mechanism breaks entirely; character stands without primary defense
  dark moment = no practiced backup; wound directly exposed; most unfamiliar state the character has inhabited

---

SCENE FRICTION (mechanism mismatch between characters)
  when two wounded characters share a scene, each runs a mechanism — the pairing generates friction
  overcompensator + avoider → pursuit dynamic; one charges, one retreats
  two overcompensators → dominance collision
  surrenderer + avoider → approach-withdrawal loop
  use at scene design stage: identify each character's active mechanism; predict the friction pattern
