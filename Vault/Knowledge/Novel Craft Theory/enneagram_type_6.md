# TYPE 6 — HEAD · anchor-seeking

WOUND: without a reliable anchor the world is genuinely dangerous and nothing internal can make it safe. Context people — identity borrowed from the machine (group/institution/cause/person/ideology).
MECH [the machine]: when the machine holds, the six holds. Identity is not free-floating — it is organized around the anchor. Narrative engine activates the moment the machine begins to prove insufficient, corrupt, or false.
MECH [inner committee]: can argue every position with genuine conviction, then argue the opposite. Formidable devil's advocates — the consideration is structural, not rhetorical. Six who relies on resolving the debate before acting will never act. The debate does not resolve; it is the nature of the type to generate it.
ARC: leap of faith. NOT a decision or conclusion of faith — a leap is irrational by nature, undertaken without the certainty they've always insisted on first. Certainty was never what they actually needed. Only the experience of surviving the leap demonstrates what they couldn't be told.
ENGINE: is this a story about leaving or a story about staying? Every scene maps as movement toward or away from the departure moment.
TENSION: identity parasitic on the anchor → remove the anchor → genuinely unanswerable "who am I" → arc requires standing alone in exactly the uncertainty they've organized their life to avoid
PHOBIC/COUNTERPHOBIC: both are fear management strategies, neither is health. Counterphobic = running toward fear offensively; phobic = accommodating it. Arc requires a different *relationship* to the fear (through it, not around it) — not just different behavior.
MECH [corner dog anger]: six anger quality = specific. Backed-into-corner anger with an undercurrent of stress, slightly panicked. ≠ gut center's pre-cognitive sovereign anger (8/9/1). ≠ heart center's relational-reactive anger (2/3/4). Comes from above the shoulders; coiled tension rather than power.
STRESS→3: anxiety unmanageable → performs competence to mask internal terror; joylessly driven, image-conscious — looks like ambition, is the anxiety gone underground
INT→9: scanning system quiets; genuine interior stability; can hold uncertainty without being destabilized

SHAPES
  redeem [3-part]: machine (earn the attachment, reader must understand why it held) → fracture (contact with reality outside the machine; two incompatible truths at once) → departure (go rogue; discover they can stand in uncertainty and not collapse)
  tragedy: machine fails → find smaller/darker/more dependent machine; any port in storm. Barry in Barry | Henry Hill in Goodfellas (stripped of every machine = living death for this type)
  ultimate: stops needing the anchor — can now choose commitments from autonomy rather than fear. "I am loyal because this is worth my loyalty" ≠ "I cannot survive without this."

ANTAG: means it — no cynical interior separate from public conviction. Authority derived from the machine, not self-validating like the one → more vulnerable, will defend machine with everything because without it they are nothing.
  dogmatic enforcer (does terrible thing because machine requires it, not personal want) | counterphobic destroyer (preemptive offensive against perceived threat) | paranoid center (closed loop; loyalty tests impossible to pass)
ANTAG weakness: demonstrate concretely that the machine is corrupt/false. Unlike the one (can only be confronted with outcomes), the machine is falsifiable. Show the six what the machine actually is.
ANTAG can be converted: loyalty is the mechanism of menace — loyalty can be redirected. If story shows the six a better cause and the six experiences it as revelation, transition is emotionally coherent.

NOT
  → anxiety ≠ the surface — can present as warm, decisive, confident, aggressive, charming. Anxiety is the basement; visible only when the management strategy breaks down.
  → "lowkey right" problem: the survival instinct is genuinely useful; in dangerous environments, sixes thrive. The flaw is harder to identify than other types'. Find the specific cost: relationship destroyed by testing, machine trusted past evidence, leap not taken because certainty was always one data point away.
  → sixes can look like almost any other type — stop looking at personality vibe; look at the pathology. Does the character's arc center on loyalty/duty/roles/systems/"who am I in relation to what structure"? Then it's six-coded regardless of surface presentation.

DX
  1. What is this character's machine — the anchor they've organized their identity around? What specific quality of it holds the weight?
  2. What early authority relationship failed them — when did the reliable thing prove not to be, and what did that cost?
  3. What does the scanning system look like in practice — what signals read as threat? Where is it accurate, where is it projecting?
  4. Phobic, counterphobic, or moving between both — what does the flip look like when it happens?
  5. What does the machine demand that they cannot give — where is the fracture, the moment when loyalty and judgment point in opposite directions?
  6. How do they handle the inner committee — do they fall into paralysis, or have they developed a strategy for moving through the noise?
  7. What would the departure look like — what is the six most terrified of losing when they imagine going rogue?
  8. How does the attachment dynamic manifest in the romantic relationship — does this character test? How? What does a failed test cost?
  9. What does their anger look like — corner dog reactive, or transformed by counterphobic processing into something offensive?
  10. When this six goes into three-stress — performing competence — what does that look like externally? Who notices first?
  11. What would genuine courage look like — not counterphobic offense, not performed confidence, but an actual leap of faith with genuinely uncertain outcome?
  12. If they found their way to integration — nine's peace, holding uncertainty without destabilization — what in their life looks different that doesn't now?
