# Antagonist Design
# source: "HOW TO WRITE AN UNFORGETTABLE VILLAIN" — Abbiee — https://youtu.be/qV6e0IqkzxU
# the antagonist as a DESIGN SUBJECT (full interiority) — distinct from the antagonist-as-force at a beat ([[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]])
# depth-tier placement: antagonist sits TOP tier alongside protagonist → [[character-profiles]] DEPTH ALLOCATION

---

DUAL MANDATE (the two obligations — and why they're ONE)
  (a) real wound model: own fear[+]desire poles, misbelief, arc — run the full engine ([[internal-conflict]])
  (b) pressure vessel: applies calibrated force to the protagonist's internal+external journey
  failure modes:
    miss (a) → hollow/cheesy villain: "bad because the plot needs it"
    miss (b) → deep villain who fails the story: interiority that never bears on the hero's wound
  these are NOT a balancing act — they should be WELDED, not balanced (see below)

THE WELD — engineer targeted contact
  invariant: the antagonist, pursuing HIS OWN goal for HIS OWN reasons, must land on the
    protagonist's PROTECTED territory (what the misbelief is guarding) — not just the surface desire
  when welded, (b) comes FREE out of (a): pressing his own agenda automatically presses the hero's wound
  geometry of contact (pole-alignment is the reliable guarantor, NOT a requirement to duplicate):
    MIRROR poles — same fear/desire, divergent misbelief→method (selfish route vs heroic route)
      e.g. George/Ross: both fatherless, both refuse to be at anyone's mercy; Ross is living proof
      George's misbelief ("buy my way above others' mercy") is a lie → that's why Ross must be destroyed
    COMPLEMENTARY poles — near-opposite, still land dead-on (control-need antagonist vs being-controlled wound)
  caution: forced exact-duplicate mirror reads TRITE ("we're not so different, you and I"). engineer
    the contact, don't duplicate the character. mirror is one option, not the mold.
  decoupled = the swappable-antagonist failure already flagged at [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]]:
    pressure present but not calibrated; no amount of "remember to use him" fixes uncalibrated poles

NEGATIVE ARC = THE PRESSURE ENGINE (descent and escalation are one curve)
  a negative arc IS the antagonist sealing deeper into the misbelief ([[internal-conflict]] negative arc)
  a misbelief sealing deeper ESCALATES the methods → the descent and the rising external pressure
    are the SAME curve (bankrupt → frame → try to hang → take the house: that escalation IS the descent)
  welded correctly you cannot dramatize the descent without escalating the pressure
  the decoupling danger (lose pressure to indulge interiority) = symptom of an arc running on a separate track

PRESSURE-VESSEL = ANTI-GLORIFICATION LICENSE
  source guardrail: explain, don't justify — backstory explains evil, never excuses it; never glorify
  the mechanism that enforces it is STRUCTURAL, not restraint: go as deep as you want, BECAUSE
    the pressure keeps escalating and the arc SEALS rather than redeems (chosen, not suffered)
  depth is safe precisely because the villain never stops costing the hero and never turns back
  (a flicker of good / a loved pet does not make him good — interiority ≠ absolution)

THINNER PARALLEL THREAD (full interiority, SUBORDINATED clock)
  spine follows the protagonist; antagonist beats are instrumented to the hero's arc, not co-equal
  pull ~4-5 beats, not all twelve — enough to read pressure as wound-expression, plus the escalation rungs:
    one ORIGIN glimpse — load-bearing wound/misbelief scene (not the full backstory)
    the PINCH points — loom → demonstrate → maximal proximity (already clocked at [[Beats/05_first_pinch_point]])
    the SEALING beat — antagonist's inverted dark-moment (chooses misbelief as truth), landing near the
      protagonist's aha so the two arcs RHYME in opposite directions
  reader-engagement runs on the [[subplots]] anticipation mechanic: "what happens when they collide?"

DIAGNOSTICS
  villain feels cheesy / plot-driven → missing (a): no wound engine underneath
  villain feels rich but inert → missing (b): wound doesn't contact the hero's protected territory; re-engineer poles
  antagonist swappable → poles uncalibrated to THIS protagonist; mirror or complement, but make contact
  villain tipping into sympathetic/glorified → arc isn't sealing OR pressure has slackened; restore both
  mirror reads on-the-nose → poles too literally duplicated; shift toward complementary contact
