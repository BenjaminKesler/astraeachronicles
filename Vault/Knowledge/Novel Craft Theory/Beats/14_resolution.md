# Resolution — Act 3 Beat 6 (optional)
# source: "How to Write a CLIMAX (for ANY Genre!)" — Abbie Emmons — https://youtu.be/3Rq8f63hVys
# authority: Vogler (Writer's Journey — "Return with the Elixir"), Truby (Anatomy of Story — "New Equilibrium"), Snyder (Save the Cat — "Final Image")
# synthesis: [[wound-model-thesis]], [[Beats/13_victory]]
# cross-references: [[Beats/12_climactic_confrontation]], [[Beats/11_aha_moment]]
# position: ~97–100% (follows Victory; optional; standalone vs. series determines design)

---

OPTIONAL BEAT
  not structurally mandatory — some stories end immediately after Victory
  abrupt endings that let the reader paint the rest are valid and sometimes more powerful
  whether to include depends on story style, genre contract, and standalone vs. series structure
  if included: keep proportionally brief; heavy lifting is complete; this beat settles, does not re-open

---

WOUND LAYER
state: post-transformation life beginning to establish; protagonist operating from dissolved-misbelief state in ordinary life; abstract desire now pursuable honestly; wound's cycle of suffering ending

  NEW EQUILIBRIUM (Truby)
    world settles into a new stable state — different from the opening equilibrium
    not only the protagonist who changed — the world around them re-stabilizes in response
    relationships, circumstances, and the protagonist's place in them all reflect the new state
    distinct from the protagonist's internal state (Victory) — this is the external world adjusting

  RETURN WITH THE ELIXIR (Vogler)
    protagonist brings something BACK to the world — wisdom, healing, changed behavior that affects others beyond themselves
    the transformation is not only a private event; it has outward consequence
    elixir form is story-specific: a changed relationship, a truth shared, a pattern broken that affected others, or simply the protagonist's presence in the world as their essential self
    diagnostic: what does the protagonist bring back? who else is affected by who they became?

  RELATIONAL COMPLETION
    reconciliation opened at Aha; began executing at Climax; completes here
    wound-driven relational distortions that ran through Acts 1–2 visibly resolved or on the path to resolution
    new relational equilibrium established — not perfectly repaired (the whiff of death stays), but genuinely different from the opening relational landscape

---

GENRE LAYER
  STANDALONE: all reader questions answered; no missing information or unanswered threads by the final page

  SERIES / EPISODIC:
    option 1: partial resolution — this novel's arc closes while series threads continue; reader satisfied by the contained arc but aware the larger story continues
    option 2: cliffhanger — swap the Victory beat for a new plot twist; defer external resolution to the next novel; series momentum maintained
      CRITICAL: the cliffhanger is plot-level only; the character arc must complete regardless; swapping Victory for a plot twist defers external resolution, not internal transformation
      if internal transformation is incomplete at the cliffhanger → arc failure, not a series hook

  FINAL IMAGE (Snyder)
    resolution's final image should answer the opening image — visual/tonal bookending
    opening image established the "old world" (protagonist in full-strength wound, comfort zone intact)
    final image establishes the "new world" (protagonist in dissolved-misbelief state, new equilibrium)
    gap between these two images IS the story's transformation made visible at the most compressed level
    design the final image in dialogue with the opening image; the contrast communicates the arc

---

FAILURE MODES
  EPILOGUE TRAP: epilogue locks in future events, limiting sequel options; if continuing the series, prefer dialogue-hinting or implication over explicit future-stating
  REOPENING: resolution introduces new conflict that undermines the Victory beat; this beat settles, does not re-open
  ELIXIR ABSENT: protagonist's transformation has no outward consequence; change remains private; world does not receive what the protagonist became
  RESOLUTION AS RECAP: restating what already landed in the Victory beat; redundant; trust the Victory to have done its work
  CLIFFHANGER ARC FAILURE: using the series cliffhanger option to defer the internal transformation; the arc must close even if external threads continue

---

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  what new equilibrium has the world settled into — how is it specifically different from the opening? (new equilibrium check)
  what does the protagonist bring back — who else is affected by who they became? (elixir check)
  does the final image answer the opening image? (Snyder bookending check)
  standalone: are all reader questions answered? (closure check)
  series: is the cliffhanger plot-level only — is the character arc complete? (series structure check)
  does this beat settle rather than reopen? (resolution function check)
  are the relational threads completing — not perfectly resolved, but genuinely different from their opening state? (relational completion check)
