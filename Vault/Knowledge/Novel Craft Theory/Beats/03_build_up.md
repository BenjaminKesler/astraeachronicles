WOUND LAYER
state: both fear layers engaged simultaneously; comfort zone band collapsed; misbelief at the crossroads; mechanism intact but visibly strained; dramatic irony at full strength; refusal stage possible across multiple scenes; tipping point approaches
  abstract desire: still subterranean — NOT established here; what becomes visible is the FEAR ARCHITECTURE, not the desire
    chain: Hook (incongruity leak) → II/Build-up (subterranean) → FPP (opaque exposure) → aha (full legibility)
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] values revelation
  THE IMPOSSIBLE CHOICE (core mechanism)
    direct consequence of II — character standing at the collapsed outer boundary
    two options, both painful; both touch the wound from different directions:
      OPTION A — retreat: return to comfort zone → surface desire abandoned; abstract desire permanently deferred (inner fear)
      OPTION B — venture out: cross outer boundary → misbelief-predicted outcomes become possible (outer fear)
        character perceives Option B as fear-safe: misbelief tells them they can win without confronting the wound
        "while still avoiding fear": protagonist believes they can get the desire AND sidestep the wound — the structural assumption Act 2 disproves
        → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] "while still avoiding fear" (named and elaborated as Act 2 engine)
    "impossible" = no painless exit; retreating and venturing both threaten the wound
    → [[internal-conflict]] TWO FEAR LAYERS; [[wound-model-thesis]] Part 3
  MISBELIEF AT THE CROSSROADS
    misbelief calculates "least painful" option — calculation is wrong by design
    tends toward retreat: external fear feels more manageable than wound re-triggering
    pain-floor exception: character picks Option B when status quo pain exceeds risk pain — not courage; lesser pain
      this is the tipping point mechanism in terse form → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] pain vs. pain (full elaboration)
    if venture out: misbelief immediately seeks a wound-safe route — the "while still avoiding fear" assumption activates
    FIRST LINK IN THE CAUSAL CHAIN: placed at the FPP when commitment is made, not here
      the Build-up is where the crossroads is faced and the refusal stage plays out
      the FPP is where the first wound-driven decision is committed to and the chain begins
      every decision from FPP forward accumulates causally toward self-caused ruin
      → [[plot-structure-outlining]] self-causer mechanism; [[internal-conflict]] STORY BEAT MAPPING; [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]]
  CHARACTER AS AGENT
    protagonist must be able to make the choice — plot cannot make it for them
    no genuine choice → no unanswered question → foreseeability loop fails to activate

  REFUSAL AS STAGE (Vogler)
    Build-up may span multiple scenes; refusal is a named phase within this arc, not a failure
    character initially moves toward Option A (retreat) — the wound-safe choice; the mechanism's default
    inaction cost accumulates: staying becomes increasingly untenable as the Build-up progresses
    refusal becomes unsustainable by end of Build-up → tipping point imminent → FPP
    this is what gives the FPP commitment its weight: the refusal was genuine; retreat was seriously considered
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] pain vs. pain (tipping point is the mechanism resolving refusal)

GENRE LAYER (constitutive)
state: impossible choice is genre-colored; world-building as consequence architecture
  GENRE PROMISE: the impossible choice must belong to this genre's world — what's at risk and what's lost must be genre-appropriate
    thriller: venture out = risk death; retreat = survive but lose everything worth having
    romance: venture out = risk heartbreak; retreat = never know love
  GLOBAL VALUE CHARGE (McKee — distinct from genre promise)
    both options in the impossible choice carry value-charge implications — the global value colors the cost of each side
    → [[Beats/02_inciting_incident]] genre layer (first value swing now coloring both options)
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] value charge shift (the swing that makes the FPP a felt structural beat)
  WORLD-BUILDING ROLE: CONSEQUENCE ARCHITECTURE
    reader must know what this world actually does to people who make either choice
    vague world consequences → weightless choice; the agonizing is not earned
    specific world consequences → the choice feels real and the weight is justified
    world-building is load-bearing here, not texture; genre is CONSTITUTIVE at this beat

---

SYMPATHY LAYER
state: sympathy deepens from "rooting for" into "aching for"
  DRAMATIC IRONY AS SYMPATHY ENGINE
    reader holds wound model from the hook; can see the trap; character cannot
    watching someone navigate a no-win situation with incomplete information = most powerful sympathy mechanism so far
    first point in the story where reader may feel more for the character than the character feels for themselves
    dramatic irony reaches maximum at FPP: reader additionally holds the structural irreversibility the character doesn't know about
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] sympathy layer
  RETREAT OPTION MUST BE GENUINELY APPEALING (Vogler)
    if staying is obviously wrong → no real impossible choice → no aching
    the hook made the comfort zone feel real and worth having; this is where that investment pays off
    secondary function: retreat's appeal seeds protagonist's belief that the door isn't closing — makes FPP's irreversibility gap structurally meaningful
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] sympathy layer + comfort zone forward dependency; [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] irreversibility
  EMOTIONAL SPECIFICITY REQUIREMENT (Maass)
    vague stakes at the impossible choice → weak sympathy response
    specific concrete detail in both options → choice lands at gut level
  SYMPATHY FAILURE AT THIS BEAT
    impossible choice appears to have an obvious solution the character ignores
    → reader stops aching; starts judging; identification collapses
    wound model prevents this: misbelief makes the obvious solution invisible to the character
    well-established wound = reader understands WHY the character can't see what they can see

RELATIONAL LAYER (the moral arc)
function: FIRST MORAL WEIGHT — the impossible choice carries a relational cost the character doesn't yet see
  retreat option: often means protecting the distorted relational status quo the mechanism has built
  venture option: means risking or disrupting those relationships in an unfamiliar way
  either choice costs someone else something — the character cannot yet register this
  reader can see that the wound-driven calculation has consequences for other people
  the "impossible" quality of the choice is partly relational: no option leaves the relationships untouched
  → [[Beats/02_inciting_incident]] relational layer (first contact now deepening into first moral weight)

  CALIBRATION
    subtext principle: relational cost of the choice lives in implication — reader infers; character doesn't see it
    embed rule: relational dimension felt within the impossible choice — not a separate relationship scene
    weight: SLIGHTLY MORE PRESENT than at the II; the pattern is beginning to be felt by the reader
    → [[Beats/01_hook]] relational layer for universal calibration principles

  RELATIONAL FAILURE MODE: MELODRAMA
    other characters explicitly naming the moral cost of the protagonist's choice before the story has earned it
    relational dimension foregrounded as the scene's purpose rather than felt as undercurrent

  FORWARD NOTE: relational function shifts at FPP from FIRST MORAL WEIGHT to COMMITMENT COST
    at Build-up: relational cost implied by the choice being faced — possible, not yet real
    at FPP: decision made — cost is accumulating; the relational consequence is locked in even if still unseen
    → [[Beats/04_first_plot_point]] relational layer

---

FAILURE MODE: PUNCHING BAG (no genuine choice)
  plot makes the choice for the character; OR obvious solution makes character look stupid rather than wounded
  no genuine choice → no unanswered question → curiosity engine fails to fire
  no credible blindspot → reader judges rather than aches
  prevention: both options must be genuinely costly; wound must be well-established before this beat

---

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
  DRAMATIZED VS. INTERNALIZED (Vogler)
    impossible choice need not be an extended visible debate
    choice can be internal, brief, or implicit — wound-level calculation is always happening regardless
    minimum requirement: reader must register that the choice is real, even if the character moves fast
  TWO TYPES OF IMPOSSIBLE CHOICE (Coyne)
    BEST BAD CHOICE: two bad options; character picks the less bad one → produces dread
    IRRECONCILABLE GOODS: two genuinely desirable options; can't have both → produces grief
    both are valid; genre and wound structure determine which type fits the story
  → [[universal-diagnostics]] foreseeability cycle (first loop activates at this beat)

---

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
  can the character make a genuine choice, or has the plot predetermined it?
  can I articulate both options and show why each touches the wound from a different direction?
  is the choice genre-colored — do I know what the world actually does to people who pick each option?
  is the retreat option genuinely appealing, or obviously wrong? (Vogler)
  which type of impossible choice is this — best bad choice or irreconcilable goods? (Coyne)
  dramatized or internalized — and is the minimum requirement met either way?
  what does the misbelief calculate as "least painful" — does the character's behavior track that logic?
  is the reader in dramatic irony — holding more wound context than the character at this beat?
