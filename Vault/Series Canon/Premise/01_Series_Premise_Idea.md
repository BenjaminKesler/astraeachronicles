## Raw Series Story Idea

A sci-fi novel series steeped in conspiracies and psychological thriller shadows. In a universe where truth and lies are unrecognizable, the human soul tears and claws at the fabric of what is seen and  exposes the truth underneath the carefully manufactured web.

Six of the galaxy's discarded find themselves called to unravel the darkness. A golden-boy officer now a defector, an exiled royal in mourning, a pirate who arms his own refugees, a spy who traded her body for cybernetic perfection, a half-breed hiding inside his own skin, and a refugee whose jokes give him cover to never look at the dark. This unlikely crew find and use the Astraea, a prototype stealth ship they don't yet know is at the heart of the mysteries.

A thousand-year-old war rages between the Vuquoi, an enlightened race whose principles are straining under its weight, and the Ka'ar, a race consumed by the image of their own superiority. Humanity, a child-like race just venturing outward, plays a dangerous game in joining the Vuquoi thinking they can use the alliance to leapfrog their technology and advance their civilization millennia ahead of schedule.

But, everyone is wrong about everything. The Ka'ar don't exist. The real war is a Vuquoi civil war, each side is misled in real time to see the other as this invented Ka'ar. The Vuquoi royal court, the Tal'Sha, has dirty hands, but is just another puppet, as is the criminal Syndicate beneath. The Ehmeya, a race of extreme recluses, believe they themselves are the architects of a great shadow empire, but the tools of their own regime are operating from their own agenda. The Aeliria, the genetically engineered enforcement arm of the shadow empire; made from the Ehmeya's own people, have kept the galaxy entwined in conflict.

The Aeliria erase perception in real time, deleting truth before the mind can hold it. Truth can't be broadcast; anything digital gets erased. The crew uncovers and saves it one un-hackable piece at a time; paper, intuition, analog; assembling an infrastructure of horrifying truth they can detonate all at once. They can't win loudly, and they can't win early. But, if they can smuggle enough truth past a system built to erase it, the manufactured war collapses, the Aeliria's grip breaks, and the galaxy is finally released to see what's real.
