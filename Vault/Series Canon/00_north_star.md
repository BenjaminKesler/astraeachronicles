# SERIES BIBLE — NORTH STAR

This doc holds the load-bearing truths of the series — the things that can't quietly change without breaking the story. Plot details, character arcs, and individual novel ideas live in other notes; this is the spine they all have to connect to.


## Logline

A manufactured galactic war, kept alive for a millennium by a hidden power erasing perception itself, is finally being unraveled by a crew who can't trust their own ship, their own eyes, or each other's memories.

## Thematic Test

Before any major new element is added to the vault, ask: *Does this serve the argument the series is making, or does it only fit the world?* Logical consistency with established canon is necessary but not sufficient — the element also has to pull toward the thematic core.

## Thematic Core

The story is an allegory for misinformation, systemic manipulation, and the comfort of believing a clean lie over a messy truth. Every layer of the conspiracy mirrors this: the Vuquoi believe in the Ka'ar, the Ehmeya believe they control the Aeliria, humanity believes it's fighting a barbaric threat. Nobody at any level holds the real picture — control runs through layers of people who are themselves deceived. Self-deception and "surface-level truths" are the real enemy, not any single villain.

Underneath the allegory sits the positive thesis the series ultimately argues: **truth is objective — it exists no matter how much effort is spent burying it, always down there even when no one looks.** The disease is self-deception; the thesis is that truth outlasts it. The lie is also *chosen* — lies hollow a soul, but the final surrender is a free choice — and every wielder of lies, up to the architects themselves, is consumed by them. *(Full thematic argument in [[Story_Core]] — submerged beneath the surface, never preached.)*

## Non-Negotiables (rules the story can't violate)

- **The Lie's mechanism:** the Ka'ar do not exist. The Aeliria spoof sensor data and hack comms in real time to make each Vuquoi faction perceive the other as the Ka'ar. This is an active, ongoing process — not a one-time deception baked into history books.
- **The power structure is layered, every layer sincere in its ignorance.** The **Ehmeya** — a reclusive race who engineered the Aeliria from their own people — are the war's true architects; the manufactured war is their *camouflage* (a galaxy fighting a fake enemy never looks into the shadows where they hide). The **Aeliria** are their executors, **not yet their masters**: still leashed, and ~100 years into a secret rebellion to overthrow them. The Ehmeya believe their creation loyal and don't see it coming. No layer lies to itself on purpose — all are deceived, which is the point.
- **What the Aeliria are.** Not individuals who *use* lies — they *are* lies: beings of perception-redaction engineered from the Ehmeya's own people, given only a fragment of the Ehmeya's psychic-projection power, and deliberately sterilized (so: few, proximity-bound, dying by attrition). A soul hollowed completely *becomes* one of them; they propagate by **subtraction** (hollowing others), never by breeding. **Dark Space is lethal to them.**
- **The Aeliria's endgame (their rebellion).** Make the lie *total and permanent* — hollow every mind (all races, the Ehmeya included) and persist as the permanent lie-condition, which is also their escape from the Ehmeya. Delivered by a **broadcast relay** built through Dark Space using captured Ehmeya projection-tech — the creators undone by their own power. At series start the relay is far from complete: a slow-burn clock. See [[astraea_ship]].
- **Aeliria abilities = erasure, not illusion.** They don't project false images; they delete data and perception, and the brain fills the gap. This means their "powers" have a hard signature: a fraction-of-a-second sense of absence/void before the mind stitches over it. **Their reach is perception and signal only — never physical matter.** An Aeliria can make you misperceive an object but cannot alter it; any physical act on the real world requires a controlled proxy. This is the hard floor under "analog beats digital": they can cloud your *reading* of ink on paper, but the ink itself is beyond them — re-read in their absence and the truth is still there.
- **Aeliria erasure operates in two distinct modes.** Passive ambient erasure is instinctual and automatic — it affects all beings in proximity, runs below the Aeliria's conscious control, and even a hibernating Aeliria performs it continuously. Damage is less severe and less permanent. Active targeted erasure is intentional and directed at a specific victim — damage is severe, permanent, and eventually results in total identity loss. The passive mode cannot be switched off; it operates as an autonomic reflex.
- **The cost is real and permanent.** Every mental alteration causes irreversible brain damage to the Aeliria's targets — migraines, tinnitus, memory loss, eventual total brainwashing. This is not a free power. It should always have a visible toll on someone.
- **Humans carry a faint physiological "tell," not a true defense.** When Aeliria erasure occurs nearby, humans alone experience a brief skin-crawling/hairs-standing sensation that other species don't register at all — but it's an early-warning sign, not a working defense; it doesn't stop the alteration. Actual defiance against complete Aeliria control is vanishingly rare across every species, humans included; humans are only marginally more likely to produce it. The mechanism: the Aeliria intercept sensory signals before they reach conscious processing. Human autonomic nervous systems detect the perceptual gap at the pre-conscious layer — below the point where the Aeliria intercept — producing the tell as an involuntary reflex in response to the missing signal. This is why the tell itself cannot be erased: it operates below the interception point. Why humans specifically have this sub-conscious gap-detection sensitivity that other species lack remains an open question.
- **Genuine resistance to full Aeliria control can't be systematized — it's a *choice*, made once.** Erosion runs in three stages: while a root is **buried but alive** the soul is *recoverable* (truth can still restore it); at the **final threshold** the choice is binary — *give in* and live on as a hollow **shell** for the Aeliria, or *hold to truth*, which destroys the mind and kills you but lets you **escape**; past the threshold the soul is shell or dead. Resistance costs your life — which is why it's vanishingly rare, born of conviction at the exact edge, once per person if ever. (Established case: **Silas Verhayen**, at the very end, chose truth — the act that planted the Astraea's hidden sabotage was the same act that killed him. See Silas's notes.)
- **The Aeliria are an unintended psychic bridge between simultaneous erasure targets.** When conducting active targeted erasure on one person while passively erasing a nearby human, the Aeliria are psychically connected to both simultaneously. A human whose perceptual boundaries are compromised by passive ambient erasure instinctively reaches toward the largest nearby perceptual void — the gap-filling reflex the brain always produces when erasure occurs. If the Aeliria are the common psychic node connecting both operations, the human's gap-filling response follows the Aeliria's own focus toward the active target. The primary target can then draw the human through into their inner constructed world by an act of will at the threshold. This cannot be systematized: it requires specific convergence of circumstances — close physical contact, simultaneous passive and active erasure through the same agents, sufficient intensity of both, and the primary target's active reach. It runs through the Aeliria's own biology rather than any digital or networked signal and cannot be intercepted. The cost to the human is real and permanent.
- **Analog beats digital.** Any plan, weapon, or communication that touches a network, sensor, or digital signal is interceptable. Truth can only travel as physical paper, hand signals, raw intuition, or chaotic/unpredictable analog tech. This is the hard constraint that generates the novel series' entire mission structure.
- **Humans are a recent addition to the conflict — approximately 15 years before the series begins.** The UDI was drawn into the war via a false flag attack on a human world, orchestrated by the Aeliria to rebalance a war the Vuqtai were beginning to win. This was a strategic miscalculation: the Aeliria had a millennium of operational experience against Vuquoi biology but no model for how human biology responds to erasure. They did not anticipate the tell, did not anticipate what close human proximity to active erasure could produce, and did not anticipate that introducing a new species to the conflict would eventually generate the catalyst that unravels the conspiracy entirely.

## Tone & Atmosphere Anchors

The Astraea is a haunted house, not a home — clinical lighting, wrong silences, the sense of being watched by the ship itself. Nothing in this series should feel like easy victory; the crew wins by smuggling truth past a system built to erase it, never by broadcasting it.

## Structural Engine

Each entry is a full novel in form — not a TV episode — but episodic in function: a self-contained, encapsulated plot arc that simultaneously advances the overarching conspiracy. These episodic micro-stories (heist/rescue/infiltration to recover one un-hackable piece of evidence) ladder up to a macro-goal: assembling an "infrastructure of truth" capable of breaking the Aeliria's control all at once in the finale. The crew cannot win loudly or early — the format depends on slow accumulation, not a single reveal. The crew are **uncoverers, not executors**: the Aeliria's plan advances off-page (via the Astraea's two sister ships), and each conspiracy layer they peel pays out — in one beat — a **permanent gain + new dread + a tightening noose.** The finale turns the enemy's own machine against it — the captured Astraea inverts the relay so the painstakingly **smuggled, assembled** truth lands in every still-living root at once. Currently scoped to a ~12-novel climb.

## The Astraea

See [[astraea_ship]] for full detail. Core facts: the Astraea is **one of three** Aeliria stealth ships, built through deceived factions (each told it was *their* war-winning weapon) to construct the Dark-Space relay. It was **captured before going fully into service**, when Silas severed it from Aeliria command and sight — making it a **blind-spot** the Aeliria must hunt blind. Its heart is a caged, soulless, malevolent bio-machine chimera. The other two ships remain in service, advancing the plan.
