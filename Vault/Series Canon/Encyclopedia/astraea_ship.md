# The Astraea — Construction, Concealment & Purpose

The Astraea is **one of three** sister stealth ships, the product of a compartmentalized black program run across multiple factions with the Aeliria orchestrating from the shadows. Each participant held only a fragment of the picture; each believed their involvement would win them the war. *(This is the T2 detail home for the ship and the relay; [[00_north_star]] holds the compressed version.)*

## What the ships are for — the relay

The three ships are the Aeliria's **construction-and-heist fleet** for their own secret rebellion (~100 years running) against their makers, the Ehmeya. Their goal: build a **broadcast relay** strung through the threads of Dark Space that will make the lie *total and permanent* — hollow every mind (all races, the Ehmeya included) and leave the Aeliria persisting as the permanent lie-condition, beyond any master's reach.

The relay needs three ingredients: the Aeliria's own erasure-essence, **captured Ehmeya psychic-projection tech** (to project at galaxy scale — and to reach even the immune Ehmeya, undoing the creators with their own power), and **Dark-Space navigation tech.** Because **Dark Space is lethal to the Aeliria**, they cannot build or operate any of this themselves — the entire endgame must run by *proxy:* stealth ships, and crews they control.

## Why it had to be built in secret

Two layers of secrecy. The mundane one: the Astraea required technology and resources that were illegal, irreversibly dangerous to acquire, and politically catastrophic to disclose — what it cost to build would turn public sentiment against the war entirely. The deeper one: this is the Aeliria's **rebellion**, and it must be hidden from the **Ehmeya** — their still-active masters, who are immune to ordinary Aeliria redaction. So the Aeliria launder the work through human/Vuquoi factions the Ehmeya don't bother watching, and the ships carry engineered (not redaction-based) stealth specifically to stay invisible to Ehmeya sensors.

## The fuel

A volatile raw material mined under illegal and lethal labor conditions — Syndicate-run camps using enslaved workers, no legitimate workforce willing to accept the danger. Powers a self-sustaining fusion core designed for centuries of continuous operation. (See Taelyn's notes — he escaped one of these camps.)

## The caged core — the heart

At the center of each ship is the Aeliria's **crude attempt to reproduce the Ehmeya's psychic-projection ability** in hardware — far weaker than the real thing, which is precisely *why* they still must heist the genuine Ehmeya tech for the relay. It is an **animalistic chimera — part human, Vuquoi, Aeliria, and machine.** Not conscious, no soul, no personhood; *alive*, but no "person" is there — and carrying an innate malevolence like the Aeliria themselves. Its **hybrid nature is what lets the Aeliria-psychic element survive Dark Space** (the human/Vuquoi/machine parts buffer the lethal-to-pure-Aeliria core). It crudely powers the ship's stealth and its crew-control.

## The technology bridge — the experiments

Bridging Aeliria-origin biology into machinery required classified **medical experimentation on human and Vuquoi subjects**, conducted by researchers who believed they were studying Ka'ar biology — the subjects almost certainly their own people, relabeled under the conspiracy's false taxonomy. This produced the caged core. A separate strand — experimentation on the Aeliria's **own kind** — produced the **Dark-Space sensor tech** that lets the ships navigate the lethal dark.

## Capabilities

- **Stealth** — engineered (tech-based) concealment to hide from the **Ehmeya** (immune to native redaction), plus redaction-based concealment against everyone else.
- **Dark-Space navigation** — the self-experiment sensor tech, but *the system alone isn't enough:* it also requires **special pilots** able to perceive through the dark (see Callum, whose hybrid biology / erasure-resistance is the navigation key).
- **Crew-control** — embedded bio-tech to control the proxy worker-crews who do the building. (This is the source of the ship's haunted, watched, crew-controlling dread — see [[aesthetic_guide]].)

## The Ehmeya-tech heist

The relay can't be completed without captured Ehmeya psychic-projection tech. At series start the Aeliria are only **just beginning** to gather it — playing the long game, moving cautiously. The escalation of that heist **coincides with the crew climbing to that layer of the conspiracy** (a heist against the immune, reclusive gods — potentially its own late-series arc).

## Layered knowledge among participants

Some deeply Aeliria-compromised individuals knew the Aeliria's true identity and believed themselves willing collaborators with a superior power. Others knew only the Syndicate connection and the program's illegal nature. Others knew only fragments. No layer held the complete picture — including the Aeliria's actual intent for the ships, which not even the Ehmeya know.

## Silas Verhayen's role & the capture

By the time of construction, Silas was deeply under Aeliria control and was used as a **conduit** for funneling Aeliria technology into the Astraea through UDI/Syndicate channels. At his **final threshold**, all but fully erased, he chose truth — and the single unrepeatable act that **severed the Astraea from Aeliria command and sight** was the same act that killed him. That sabotage is why the ship could be **stolen and held**, and why the Aeliria must **hunt it blind** (a ghost / blind-spot). The Astraea was thus **captured before going fully into service.** Its two sisters remain in service, advancing the plan off-page — the series' ambient clock. (See Alistair's notes for the broader Silas thread.)

## The Tal'Sha's involvement

The Tal'Sha monarchy participated in the Syndicate operation, justifying it as a necessary measure to win the war. Their hands are genuinely dirty — criminal collaboration, not passive complicity — but they too were manipulated. They did not know the Aeliria were the program's true architects. (Karae's brother was erased for getting too close to this connection — see Karae's notes.)
