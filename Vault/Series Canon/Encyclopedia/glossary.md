# Glossary

All established terms, factions, technologies, and concepts used across the series. Entries marked **[NEEDS DEFINITION]** are used in existing notes but not yet defined.

---

## Aeliria
The true architects of the war. A species capable of erasing data and perception — they do not project false images but delete sensory information, letting the brain fill the gap. Genetically engineered by the Ehmeya, but have since infiltrated and now puppet their creators. The Ehmeya believe they are the ones pulling strings; they are not. See North Star Non-Negotiables for the full ruleset governing their abilities.

## The Astraea
A prototype stealth ship — the Aeliria's master key for the next generation of their control infrastructure. Built secretly across multiple factions via Syndicate channels, with each participant holding only a fragment of the full picture. See [[astraea_ship]] for full construction and concealment detail.

## Dark Space
A region of space heavily redacted by the Aeliria. Humanity and the Vuquoi mistakenly believe Dark Space is simply vast expanses containing no star systems. The Aeliria have also done something to the space itself to make navigation extremely difficult for anyone but themselves. Recurs as a travel/plot device across the season rather than a single location tied to one event.

**Open question:** The exact mechanism by which the Aeliria make Dark Space difficult to navigate is not yet established — needs further development.

## Ehmeya
A shadow-dwelling species that created the Aeliria. They believe they are running a shadow empire through the Aeliria; in reality, the Aeliria have infiltrated and now control them. They are sincere in their ignorance — not lying to themselves on purpose.

## Ghost Channel
A covert communication method: a message formatted to exactly mimic a known dead intelligence network, slipping past most surveillance because it looks like routine correspondence in an already-established pattern. The Aeliria can detect it precisely because they killed the network themselves — a dead channel carrying current is the anomaly their monitoring flags. (Established use: the UDI contact reaching Karae after her brother's death.)

## Ka'ar
The fabricated "third race" — each Vuquoi faction believes it is fighting the Ka'ar, when in reality they are fighting each other, with the Aeliria spoofing sensor data and hacking comms in real time to sustain the illusion. The Ka'ar do not exist.

**Ka'ar (perceived appearance):** No human or Vuquoi has ever encountered a Ka'ar in person. When seen over screens, the Aeliria alter the video stream in real time to display a large, 7-foot-tall lizard-like creature with monstrous claws and teeth.

## Lancer
A class of small, single-person fighter spacecraft. Callum's father was a Lancer pilot and squadron leader.

## Neuro-Dampener
A black-market drug that physically suppresses Vuquoi biological markers. Used by Callum to hide his mixed human/Vuquoi heritage while living in a hyper-nationalist human military sector. Side effects include dulled Vuquoi intuition and memory loss.

## SAVI AI Chip
Shaye's integrated AI chip — tailors her memories and tracks the Astraea on behalf of the Syndicate. She believes disconnecting it will render her helpless; her arc disproves this. Full capabilities beyond tracking and memory tailoring are not yet established.

## The Syndicate
A multi-faction criminal organization used by the Aeliria as an operational intermediary. Runs illegal black-market operations including the enslaved labor camps that mine the Astraea's fuel. Operates front businesses (e.g., the Syndicate-front bar on the Vuquoi home world where Karae's contact was erased). The Tal'Sha monarchy participated in Syndicate operations for the Astraea's construction program, believing it a necessary wartime measure. Shaye worked as a Syndicate-embedded spy. The Syndicate does not know it is being puppeted — it is one more layer of the conspiracy that is sincere in its ignorance.

## Tal'Sha Monarchy
The main Vuquoi faction, formally allied with humanity (UDI). Rules through a royal court. Participated in the Syndicate's black program to build the Astraea, believing it would win them the war; did not know the Aeliria were the program's true architects. Their hands are genuinely dirty — criminal collaboration, not passive complicity.

## UDI (United Defense Initiative)
The human faction. Drawn into the war approximately 15 years before the series begins via a false flag attack on a human world, orchestrated by the Aeliria to rebalance a conflict the Vuqtai were beginning to win. Formally allied with the Tal'Sha.

## Vuquoi
A spacefaring race split into two factions — the Tal'Sha Monarchy (main faction) and the Vuqtai (rebel faction) — each of which believes the other is the Ka'ar. The war between them is the Aeliria's primary mechanism of control.

## Vuqtai
The rebel Vuquoi faction. At war with the Tal'Sha, perceived by Tal'Sha as the Ka'ar (and vice versa). Were beginning to win the conflict approximately 15 years before the series begins — the strategic position that prompted the Aeliria to draw humanity into the war via a false flag attack.
