# Aesthetic Guide — Astraea Chronicles

> **DRAFT (2026-06-29).** Living file — grounded against canon, governance settled; tangible content still to be built.
>
> **What this file is:** the world's *sensory identity* — what Astraea looks and feels like, and how that feel encodes the theme. A **check, not a recipe**: it doesn't dictate how a scene looks; it lets character/scene/beat generate the texture, then checks that texture against the axis. Distinct from a future **prose-style** doc (the page-level *language* used to render sensory detail — *how* a scene is written), which is a separate job.

## Core principle — grounding is a floor, not a quota

The look is the visible consequence of a canon rule: digital truth gets erased, so truth-dealers must live in analog. Aesthetic-as-consequence reads as authentic; aesthetic-as-decoration reads as set-dressing.

But grounding **constrains**, it does not **mandate.** The axis is a line the aesthetic may never *contradict* — not a quota every choice must *fill.* Above that floor, the aesthetic runs free: specific colors, quality of light, lived-in particulars that mean nothing thematically and exist purely to make the world real. Forcing every micro-choice to earn a thematic paycheck builds a diagram, not a world (and violates soul-over-correctness). The rule is negative space: *don't fight the truth/lie reading; above that, be flavorful.* (Roots in north-star's "analog beats digital" non-negotiable.)

## The axis — concealment/erasure vs. persistence/exposure

The real axis is **function, not material.** What puts a thing on the lie-pole isn't whether it's *technological* — it's whether it's being used to **hide or erase the real.** Digital just *correlates* (erasable = hideable), so analog/digital is the axis's usual **costume**, not its definition. This is why a cyborg hero (Shaye, Taelyn) isn't villainous: cybernetics are lie-pole only when they're a flight *from* the real self. The poles, with their default costume:

- **Conceal / erase — the manufactured surface (the lie); default costume: digital/high-tech:** seamless, frictionless, perfect, untrustworthy, impermanent. Light, smoothness, immediacy; things that vanish without a trace.
- **Persist / expose — the underlayer (the truth); default costume: analog:** rough, effortful, physical, permanent, real. Weight, friction, smell, persistence; things that can't be reached into and deleted.

## Function — texture as compass

Sensory texture grounds a scene in truth/lies space *before any plot fact is stated.* Drift toward the lie → smoother, more seamless. Closing on truth → heavier, more physical. The reader navigates the theme through the senses.

**The wiggle room is variable.** In most scenes texture carries no signal, so flavor runs wide open. But where texture *is* doing compass-work — where smoothness is meant to *mean* drift-toward-lie — decorative flourishes that blur that reading cost the signal. So the room is wide in atmosphere-scenes and narrows when the texture is the message.

## Reframe — analog is *contraband*, not nostalgia

Not retro/vintage cool, not warmth. It's the material culture of an underground hiding truth from a system that deletes it. Paper = contraband. A handwritten ledger = a weapon (can't be edited from orbit). Ink = defiance. "Retro" → "the texture of resistance" is what keeps it from going generic.

## Structural rhyme

Surface-lie over rough-truth = the nested-reveal plot structure made *visible.* The same idea expressed in plot, theme, and now texture — three layers — which is the proof it's load-bearing, not decorative.

## The ship as the digital pole, lived-in

The Astraea is the digital/surface pole made into a "home" the crew can't trust (north-star's "haunted house, clinical lighting"). The horror is living *inside the enemy's medium*; the crew's analog tools are the truth-pole smuggled within it.

## The lie-pole's sensory signature — the ship interior

The most concentrated expression of the lie-pole is the Astraea's interior: the dread of living *inside the enemy's medium.* Its texture (the erasure-signature, the haunted-house feel):

- **Watched-ness, and movement is the trigger.** A pervasive sense of being watched even in an empty ship; the felt rule is that *the eyes track movement* — so the body instinctively stills, whispers, moves slow. (Echoes the human tell — hair standing on the neck — as a constant low-grade hum.)
- **Neither the dark nor the light is safe.** The dark is the unseen watcher; the light is worse — you can't trust what you see, sensing an *off-ness* you can't name (the void the mind stitches over). Cold **monitor-blue** is the lie-pole's signature light; a low **drone/hum** its ambient sound.
- **Second-order dread — the fear of your own ease.** A flicker of calm, then a corner-of-eye primal fear, then a *greater* fear: that you let your guard down. The ship *wants* you complacent; relaxing feels like betrayal and danger at once.
- **The void made tactile.** Reach into the wrong room and your hand brushes something that *moves* — you recoil, and nothing is there. The absence has presence.
- **The only trust is each other.** The irrational fear of the ship is answered only by the irrational comfort of the crew's presence — and *that*, not logic, is the safe thing. (The series thesis dramatized at the smallest scale: where your own perception can't be trusted, collective human witness is the only ground.)

## Guardrail — characters (and places) don't resolve cleanly onto one pole

Falls out of the function-not-material axis above: digital isn't *evil*, it's *erasable*; analog isn't *holy*, it's *un-erasable.* The cyborg spy — a high-tech person fighting for analog truth — is the walking proof. Don't sort any character or place cleanly onto one pole; the interesting ones straddle.

## Settled & open

Canon grounding done — every main character already embodies this axis as psychology, and their arcs walk from the lie-pole toward the truth-pole (texture may shift with arc-stage).
