# Aesthetic Guide — Astraea Chronicles

> **DRAFT (rev. 2026-07-01).** Living file — grounded against canon, governance settled; the structural skeleton (emotional engine + the plane) is in — tangible per-corner texture still to be built.
>
> **What this file is:** the world's *sensory identity* — what Astraea looks and feels like, and how that feel encodes the theme. A **check, not a recipe**: it doesn't dictate how a scene looks; it lets character/scene/beat generate the texture, then checks that texture against the axis. Distinct from a future **prose-style** doc (the page-level *language* used to render sensory detail — *how* a scene is written), which is a separate job.

## The emotional engine — the un-deletable

Beneath the axis is the feeling that makes it matter, and it must stay warm — compressing it into a rule kills the thing it names. Effort is self-transfer: you give part of yourself to make a thing, and the more it cost, the more of you is in it. A made thing is a piece of the self set outside the body — and if it can't be reached into and deleted, neither can the self in it. **The un-deletable makes you feel more un-deletable.** In a world whose antagonist erases people, a permanent mark is how a person refuses to be erased: self-witness at the smallest scale.

Design consequence: this world does not fetishize the *object* (the handsome switch, the honest machine — that road dead-ends in cassette-futurism). It fetishizes **the mark a person left on the object** — the scratch, the wear, the note carved because the ship couldn't be trusted to hold it. The fingerprint, not the fixture.

## Core principle — grounding is a floor, not a quota

The look is the visible consequence of a canon rule: digital truth gets erased, so truth-dealers must live in analog. Aesthetic-as-consequence reads as authentic; aesthetic-as-decoration reads as set-dressing.

But grounding **constrains**, it does not **mandate.** The axis is a line the aesthetic may never *contradict* — not a quota every choice must *fill.* Above that floor, the aesthetic runs free: specific colors, quality of light, lived-in particulars that mean nothing thematically and exist purely to make the world real. Forcing every micro-choice to earn a thematic paycheck builds a diagram, not a world (and violates soul-over-correctness). The rule is negative space: *don't fight the truth/lie reading; above that, be flavorful.* (Roots in north-star's "analog beats digital" non-negotiable.)

## The plane — two axes, four corners

The sensory identity resolves onto a plane of two orthogonal axes; texture places a thing on it *before any plot fact is stated.*

**Vertical — the self (truth vs. lie).** Function, not material: what matters is not whether a thing is *technological* but whether it is used to hide/erase the real or to persist/expose it. Digital merely *correlates* (erasable = hideable), so analog/digital is the axis's usual **costume**, not its definition — which is why a cyborg fighting *for* analog truth isn't villainous; cybernetics are lie-pole only when they're a flight *from* the real self.
- **Erase (lie) — costume digital/high-tech:** seamless, frictionless, perfect, untrustworthy, impermanent; self absent.
- **Persist (truth) — costume analog:** rough, effortful, physical, permanent, real; self present.

**Horizontal — the eye (surveilled vs. witnessed).** Not safety — *which gaze you live under.* The erasing eye you must hide from, vs. the witnessing eye that sees you into realness. (Supersedes an earlier "freedom/safety" framing; *safety* was the lossy word — a thing can move toward *witnessed* while becoming **less** safe. Roots in the ship-dread thesis below: collective human witness is the only ground.)
- **Surveilled:** hidden from the eye — furtive, concealed, made under duress.
- **Witnessed:** before the eye — shown, claimed, held.

**The four corners** — names are the retrieval keys; hold the vocabulary:
- **Contraband** (truth + surveilled): full self, hidden. Rough, made in duress — the journal under the floorboard.
- **Sanctuary** (truth + witnessed): full self, shown and held. Refined-but-human craft, flaws as beauty — the claimed room, the converted ship; the destination arcs climb toward.
- **Monument** (lie + witnessed): self absent, displayed for status. Luxurious seamless perfection, owned-not-made — a mausoleum of absent personhood.
- **Cipher** (lie + surveilled): self absent, hidden and deniable. Utilitarian seamlessness — the false-bottom, the forgettable tool that erases its own traces.

**Coherence / distinctness:** both axes read consistently across all four corners — vertical always self present/absent; horizontal always hidden-from vs. before-the-eye, its meaning decaying cleanly as the self drops out: *witnessed* falls from **held** (sanctuary) to mere **display** (monument), *surveilled* from **contraband** (a self worth hiding) to **deniable cipher** (nothing to hide but a function). The two cold corners are not one grey smear: **monument is ostentatious erasure, cipher is invisible erasure** — show-perfect vs. vanish-perfect.

**Corners in motion.** The corners aren't cells to file things into; characters and spaces *travel* between them. The load-bearing adjacency: **contraband → sanctuary is the same objects, opposite frame** — contraband is *dangerous every time you enter*, sanctuary is *somewhere you can live.* The whole difference is the frame around the objects, not the objects: the danger lives in the *frame* (the watching, the clock, the concealment), so when the frame drops, contraband becomes sanctuary without a single object changing. Corollary — keep the frame's teeth: the moment safety stops being *metered* (the counted absence, the clock), contraband collapses into cozy and loses its charge.

## Function — texture as compass

Sensory texture grounds a scene in truth/lies space *before any plot fact is stated.* Drift toward the lie → smoother, more seamless. Closing on truth → heavier, more physical. The reader navigates the theme through the senses.

**The wiggle room is variable.** In most scenes texture carries no signal, so flavor runs wide open. But where texture *is* doing compass-work — where smoothness is meant to *mean* drift-toward-lie — decorative flourishes that blur that reading cost the signal. So the room is wide in atmosphere-scenes and narrows when the texture is the message.

## Perfection vs. craftsmanship — why cold is cold

In *made* things the tell between the poles is whether the making is preserved or erased. **Perfection is the erasure of process** — a seamless surface that hides the hand and presents as if it sprang into being without cost: the antagonist's own gesture applied to craft, redaction with good taste. **Craftsmanship preserves the process** — you can read the hand, the decisions, the effort; the object remembers being made, so the self that made it is still in it.

The word *perfection* does two jobs — split them: **seamlessness** (flawless to appear *without origin* — cold) vs. **mastery** (flawless as the visible summit of a person's effort — warm). Both flawless; opposite poles. So refinement is *not* the tell — flawless-but-owned art is **monument** precisely because the relation to it is *ownership, not making*, and self cannot be transferred into a thing commissioned to impress. The truth-pole's clearest single object: **kintsugi** — a break mended in gold, the fracture made the most visible thing. The opposite of erasure: *I was broken, a hand mended me, and I will not pretend otherwise.*

## Reframe — analog is *contraband*, not nostalgia

Not retro/vintage cool, not warmth. It's the material culture of an underground hiding truth from a system that deletes it. Paper = contraband. A handwritten ledger = a weapon (can't be edited from orbit). Ink = defiance. "Retro" → "the texture of resistance" is what keeps it from going generic.

## Structural rhyme

Surface-lie over rough-truth = the nested-reveal plot structure made *visible.* The same idea expressed in plot, theme, and now texture — three layers — which is the proof it's load-bearing, not decorative.

## The ship as the digital pole, lived-in

The Astraea is the digital/surface pole made into a "home" the crew can't trust (north-star's "haunted house, clinical lighting"). The horror is living *inside the enemy's medium*; the crew's analog tools are the truth-pole smuggled within it.

## The lie-pole's sensory signature — the ship interior

The most concentrated expression of the lie-pole is the Astraea's interior: the dread of living *inside the enemy's medium.* Its texture (the erasure-signature, the haunted-house feel):

- **Watched-ness, and movement is the trigger.** A pervasive sense of being watched even in an empty ship; the felt rule is that *the eyes track movement* — so the body instinctively stills, whispers, moves slow. (Echoes the human tell — hair standing on the neck — as a constant low-grade hum.)
- **Neither the dark nor the light is safe.** The dark is the unseen watcher; the light is worse — you can't trust what you see, sensing an *off-ness* you can't name (the void the mind stitches over). Cold **monitor-blue** is the lie-pole's signature light; a low **drone/hum** its ambient sound.
- **Second-order dread — the fear of your own ease.** A flicker of calm, then a corner-of-eye primal fear, then a *greater* fear: that you let your guard down. The ship *wants* you complacent; relaxing feels like betrayal and danger at once.
- **The void made tactile.** Reach into the wrong room and your hand brushes something that *moves* — you recoil, and nothing is there. The absence has presence.
- **The only trust is each other.** The irrational fear of the ship is answered only by the irrational comfort of the crew's presence — and *that*, not logic, is the safe thing. (The series thesis dramatized at the smallest scale: where your own perception can't be trusted, collective human witness is the only ground.)

## The truth-pole in the field — contraband texture

The truth-pole's field expression — the counterpart to the ship-interior lie-signature above. Where the lie-pole is seamless and odorless, contraband is dense, effortful, sensory. Its signature (from a scene-immersion pass, T2 skin):

- **Interstitial.** It lives in the ship's *negative space* — wiring conduits, plumbing runs, the gaps between armor and structure, framing never meant to hold a person. Truth inhabits the seams of the enemy's own architecture; nothing is built, the unclaimed is claimed.
- **Scavenged / leaked.** It runs on the system's spillover — gravity that leaks into some nooks and not others (zero-G navigated by pulling along ducting), everything made from the ship's own guts.
- **Light: brought flame vs. leaked glow.** A low living flame *carried in and extinguished on the way out,* against the ship's garish official light and the faint cold glow of leaked energy conduits. Fire vs. monitor-blue. Warmth is something you *bring*, not something the space holds.
- **Ink from the machine.** Homemade, imperfect, iterated, smelling of bearing grease — carbon and lubricant rendered from the enemy's own body. Truth's material scavenged from the thing it defies.
- **The metered clock.** A wind-up spring alarm on a chain: the eye can't see *into* the nook, but it counts your *absence.* Selfhood is stolen, rationed time — always ending. The anti-cozy object made concrete.
- **The mark is also a liability.** The most human act — writing yourself onto a page — leaves evidence (ink-stains → gloves before an inspection). The warm self-act comes pre-wrapped in cold concealment-craft.
- **Write-against-confabulation.** Because the Aeliria can only *erase* a perceived word and let the mind fill the blank (north-star: *"erasure, not illusion — the brain fills the gap"*), contraband writing is built *over-determined* — the same fact said three ways (numeral / word / tally), encoded relationally ("dock 17 — four past the fuel bay") — so a redaction can't be smoothly filled, only leave a *visible wound.* Not encryption: writing designed so truth can't be quietly subtracted. Doubles as the crew's later **verification** discipline (re-read in the Aeliria's absence; cross-check between people) once they learn *why* the inherited habit works.

## Guardrail — characters (and places) don't resolve cleanly onto one pole

Falls out of the function-not-material axis above: digital isn't *evil*, it's *erasable*; analog isn't *holy*, it's *un-erasable.* The cyborg spy — a high-tech person fighting for analog truth — is the walking proof. Don't sort any character or place cleanly onto one pole; the interesting ones straddle.

## Settled & open

**Settled (the skeleton — a T1 lens, kept compact):** the emotional engine (self-transfer / the un-deletable / fingerprint-not-fixture); the plane — two axes (self × the eye) with its four named corners (**contraband / sanctuary / monument / cipher**); function not material, analog/digital as costume; perfection = erasure of process vs. craftsmanship = its preservation; grounding a *floor, not a quota*; the guide a *check, not a recipe*.

**Open / next (the skin — T2 detail, meant to breathe):** the tangible per-corner texture — color palette, materials, quality of light, sound design — for each corner; thinnest at **cipher** (concept and homes exist, texture least rendered), plus the recognizably "Astraea" particulars beyond the ship-interior dread already built above. Each byproduct passes two gates: grounded in existing canon **and** serves north-star's Thematic Test. Keep this layer warm and unhurried — over-compaction risk lives here.
