💨 Callum: The Masked Navigator

> _"If they look at me, they see an outcast. If they look through me, I survive. I just need a place where I can belong without being caught."_

- **Enneagram:** **Type 6 (The Loyalist) with a 5 Wing**
- **The Overarching Theme Mirror:** He actively performs a biological and psychological "absence" on himself to hide from the world, mimicking the Aeliria's erasure of reality.
- **Backstory & History:** A mixed-race human/Vuquoi hiding in a hyper-nationalist human military sector. He survived by using a black-market neuro-dampener drug that physically suppresses his Vuquoi biological markers, living in constant survival panic. His father was a Lancer pilot and squadron leader who died holding the line against a Vuqtai attack on a human world — perceived by all parties as a Ka'ar attack, per the Aeliria's active spoofing — the same attack that pulled humanity into the war and led to the founding of the UDI between the Humans and the Vuquoi Tal'Sha. Callum's feelings about him are unresolved and contradictory: pride in his father's honor, lasting hurt that he chose his squadron over coming home, real respect for what the UDI was trying to build, and an unflinching, ugly understanding of what duty actually asks of the people inside it.
- **The Internal Lie / Misbelief:** _"If anyone discovers what I truly am, they will abandon me — and even the ones who don't, the ones who love me, will choose the mission over me eventually. That's what people who matter do. I can survive that, as long as I stay small, keep hiding, and never let myself actually need anyone."_
- **The Vulnerable Virtue (Why Readers Love Him):** He is the ultimate reluctant hero. He is terrified, sweating, and suffering from drug withdrawal, but his deepest fear of abandonment drives him to become the protective, obsessive "fixer" of the crew. He views this broken crew as his new pack and will do anything to keep them alive.
- **The Core Conflict & Exploitation:** His natural Type 6 anxiety makes him the perfect target for gaslighting. The neuro-dampener drug actively dulls his Vuquoi intuition and causes memory loss, making him question his own senses when he catches glimpses of the Aeliria's "Absence." He carries marginally elevated resistance to Aeliria erasure compared to full humans — above the baseline human tell, well short of rare full defiance — likely a consequence of his hybrid biology.
- **The Turning Point & Breaking the Illusion:** By the time this moment arrives, the crew already knows what he is — that reveal happens earlier, in a separate crisis still being mapped out. What's left to confront isn't exposure, it's the deeper belief underneath it. When a later crisis demands someone hold a line alone so the rest of the crew can get out, Callum takes the position the way his father once did: fully unmasked, running on raw Vuquoi instinct with nothing left to dampen, genuinely believing he won't make it back. In that moment he says the truth about his father out loud — maybe for the first time in his life — not as a performance, but because he believes there's no one left to hide it from. Someone breaks from the plan and comes back for him anyway, at real cost to the mission and the crew.
- **The Ultimate Epiphany:** Trust doesn't have to end in abandonment. His father chose the squadron and never came home; Callum makes the same kind of choice and survives, because people choose to come back for him. Security was never about hiding what he is — it was about believing that needing people wouldn't get him left behind. This time, it doesn't.
